    1. Introduction and Summary
             a. Purpose of Report and Study Objectives
                 The City of Arden Hills is developing a Guiding Plan for the B2
                 District. The purpose of this report is to determine if existing
                 transportation infrastructure can accommodate the redevelopment
                 contemplated in the Guiding Plan and to recommend changes to the
                 transportation infrastructure to enhance the redevelopment.

                 The study objectives are:
                     i. Determine how the existing County Road E/Hamline Avenue
                        West Ramp, County Road E/Connelly Avenue, County Road
                        E/Pine Tree Drive, County Road E/Holiday Inn Site Driveway,
                        County Road E/Arden Plaza Driveway, County Road
                        E/Lexington Avenue, and Lexington Avenue/Harriet Avenue
                        intersections currently operate.
                    ii. Determine if the above intersections will operate acceptably
                        after the redevelopment is fully occupied in 2030.
                   iii. Recommend improvements if needed.

             b. Executive Summary
                 The City of Arden Hills is planning for redevelopment along the
                 County Road E corridor. The redevelopment is planned to occur on
                 three different sites along the corridor: (1) the Pot `O Gold bingo hall
                 site will be replaced with 15,000 square feet of offices, (2) the Holiday
                 Inn hotel will be replaced with 95 apartment units and 120,000
                 square feet of office, and (3) the 46,000 square feet of offices on the
                 Arden Plaza site will be replaced with a 14,400 square foot pharmacy
                 and 5,000 square foot shopping center.

                 To determine if there will be significant traffic impacts to the County
                 Road E corridor, the following intersections are analyzed in this
                 study:
                       County Road E & Hamline Avenue West Ramp
                       County Road E & Hamline Avenue East Ramp/Connelly
                         Avenue
                       County Road E & Pine Tree Drive
                       County Road E & Holiday Inn Site Driveway
                       County Road E & Arden Plaza Driveway
                       County Road E & Lexington Avenue
                       Lexington Avenue & Harriet Avenue

                 The principal findings of this study are:
                       i. The roadways and study intersections within the study area
                           currently operate acceptably at Level of Service (LOS) D or
                           better and will continue to operate at LOS D or better
                           through the 2030 Build scenario when the redevelopment
                           is anticipated to be fully occupied.
                      ii. The driveways on County Road E for the Holiday Inn site
                           and the Arden Plaza site will experience significant delay
                           and queuing in the p.m. peak hour. This is acceptable as
                           an interim situation because little queuing is anticipated on
                           County Road E to pose a safety problem. Long term, the
                           queuing will impact the vitality of the redevelopments and
                           could lead to safety problems. These driveways should be
                           realigned midway between Pine Tree Drive and Lexington
                           Avenue to provide a four legged intersection that could be
                           controlled with a traffic signal. Left turn lanes and shared
                           through/right lanes should be provided on the north and
                           south legs to provide adequate egress.
                     iii.  No improvements are needed to facilitate the Pot `O Gold
                           site redevelopment.
                     iv. The County Road E corridor has a wide center two way left
                           turn lane down the middle as well as many driveway
                           intersections. This leads to a vehicle dominated corridor
                           that is unfriendly to pedestrians and bicycles. It is
                           envisioned County Road E will operate as a vibrant, multi-
                           modal corridor that will attract pedestrians and bicycles.
                           To provide a safe multi-modal corridor, it is recommended
                           County Road E should ultimately be reconstructed from
                           Hamline Avenue to Lexington Avenue to provide a
                           landscaped median, bike lanes, boulevards, and
                           sidewalks.

    3. Analysis of Existing Conditions
             a. Physical Characteristics
                 Each of the three redevelopment sites is being planned at a
                 conceptual level. The beginning assumption is that they will utilize
                 the existing access configurations.

                 The three major roadways within the study area, all designated as
                 minor arterials, are County Road E (Ramsey County State Aid
                 Highway 15), Lexington Avenue (Ramsey County State Aid Highway
                 51), and Hamline Avenue (Minnesota Trunk Highway 51). The rest
                 of the roadways within the study area are city streets. Existing traffic
                 control, speed limits, and travel lanes are shown on Figure 3.1 for
                 each study intersection. No roadway improvements are scheduled
                 near the site.

                 Metro Transit routes 225 and 261 travel along County Road E
                 through the study area and on Lexington Avenue north of the study
                 area. Route 261 also travels along Lexington Avenue through the
                 study area. There are no major bus stops within the study area, but
                 bus service is available during the day time hours.

                 There are intermittent trails and sidewalks for pedestrians through the
                 study area. The sidewalks along County Road E are interrupted by
                 many driveways that serve the local businesses. There are bicyclists
                 who use the corridor both on street and along the trails/sidewalks.
                 The County Road E/Hamline Avenue interchange provides a
                 significant barrier for pedestrians and bicycles who want to move
                 along the County Road E corridor. The bridge over Hamline Avenue
                 does not have sidewalks or on street bicycle lanes.

             b. Traffic Volumes
                 In commercial/retail areas, such as the County Road E corridor, the
                 p.m. peak hour is typically more congested than the a.m. peak
                 period. For this reason, the analysis in this study focuses on the p.m.
                 peak hour. Figure 3.2 shows the existing p.m. peak hour turning
                 movement counts for the study area. The data for the street
                 intersections was collected in 2008 and the turning movement count
                 data is contained in fifteen minute intervals in the Appendix. The
                 turning movement volume data for the County Road E/Arden Plaza
                 driveway intersection is from the 2007 Traffic and Parking Study for
                 Proposed Redevelopment of Arden Plaza. No turning movement
                 volumes are shown for the Holiday Inn site because it is currently
                 vacant and will be completely redeveloped.

    4. Projected Traffic
             a. Site Traffic Forecasting
                 A trip generation analysis was performed for the site based on the
                 methods and rates published in the ITE Trip Generation Manual, 7th
                 Edition. The weekday p.m. peak hour trip generation is for the "one
                 hour of adjacent street traffic" from 7-9 p.m. The resultant trip
                 generations are shown in Table 4.1.

                 The directional orientation of the generated traffic is shown in Figure
                 4.1. This orientation is based on a weighted distribution of the traffic
                 volumes currently using the area roadways.

                 The p.m. peak hour and daily trips generated by the site were
                 assigned to the area roadways per the trip distributions shown in
                 Figure 4.1. The traffic volumes added to the study roadways through
                 this process are shown in Figures 4.2 and Figure 4.3. Figure 4.2
                 and 4.3 are the traffic added by the development in the p.m. peak
                 hour and daily conditions respectively.

             b. Non-site Traffic Forecasting
                 As shown in Table 3.1, the daily traffic volumes on County Road E
                 and Lexington Avenue within the study area have not grown over the
                 last twenty years. Based on the flat growth in daily traffic volumes
                 within the study area, it is assumed their will be little growth in traffic
                 volumes within the study area that is not directly attributed to the
                 proposed redevelopment sites. Traffic forecasts were developed for
                 the year 2030 No-Build scenario by rounding the existing volumes to
                 represent the level of accuracy of the future forecasts. The resulting
                 2030 No-Build forecasts are shown in Figures 4.4 and 4.5.

             c. Total Traffic for 2030
                 Traffic forecasts were developed for the year 2030 Build scenario by
                 adding the traffic generated by the proposed development, as shown
                 in Figures 4.2 and 4.3, to the No-Build volumes shown in Figures 4.4
                 and 4.5. The resultant 2030 Build traffic forecasts are shown in
                 Figures 4.6 and 4.7.

             b. Long Range Improvements
                 Even though the roadways and intersections will operate within traffic
                 engineering standards, it will be difficult for vehicles to leave the
                 Holiday Inn and Arden Plaza sites during the evening rush hour. To
                 assist the redevelopment efforts, it is recommended the Arden Plaza
                 and Holiday Inn driveways be realigned midway between Pine Tree
                 Drive and Lexington Avenue. Left turn lanes and shared
                 right/through lanes should be built on the new north and south legs of
                 the intersection to provide adequate egress and the intersection
                 should be controlled with a traffic signal. The signal should be
                 installed when the traffic volumes at the intersection meet the criteria
                 for installing a traffic signal per the Minnesota Manual on Uniform
                 Traffic Control Devices. These improvements are shown
                 schematically on Figure 5.1.

                 When the traffic signal is installed at the new intersection it will
                 operate at LOS B in the 2030 Build p.m. peak hour. This leaves
                 capacity to accommodate significantly more redevelopment as long
                 as the traffic from the redevelopment is focused at the signalized
                 intersection.

    6. Conclusions and Recommendations
    The roadways and study intersections within the study area currently operate
    acceptably at Level of Service (LOS) D or better in the weekday p.m. peak hour
    and will continue to operate at LOS D or better through the 2030 Build scenario
    when the redevelopment is anticipated to be fully occupied. The driveways on
    County Road E for the Holiday Inn site and the Arden Plaza site will experience
    significant delay and queuing in the p.m. peak hour. This is acceptable as an
    interim situation because little queuing is anticipated on County Road E to pose
    a safety problem. Long term, the queuing will impact the vitality of the
    redevelopments and could lead to safety problems. These driveways should
    be realigned midway between Pine Tree Drive and Lexington Avenue to
    provide a four legged intersection that could be controlled with a traffic signal.

    The County Road E corridor has a wide center two way left turn lane down the
    middle as well as many driveway intersections. This leads to a vehicle
    dominated corridor that is unfriendly to pedestrians and bicycles. It is
    envisioned County Road E will operate as a vibrant, multi-modal corridor that
    will attract pedestrians and bicycles. To provide a safe multi-modal corridor, it
    is recommended County Road E should ultimately be reconstructed from
    Hamline Avenue to Lexington Avenue to provide a landscaped median, bike
    lanes, boulevards, and sidewalks.
