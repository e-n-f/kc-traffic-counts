I.      INTRODUCTION
Serenity Ridge is located between Arapahoe Road and Smoky Hill Road, west of the future
Powhaton Road extension in Aurora, Colorado. Figure 1 provides a vicinity map for the
proposed site.

The current filing includes 142 proposed single family homes to be located east of Oak Hill Way.
A site plan with Filing 2 highlighted in shown in Figure 2. As shown, the development will have
access onto Oak Hill Way and an access onto Smoky Hill Road.

The purpose of this study is to assess the traffic impacts on the adjacent roadways related to
the proposed development. This report includes information on existing traffic conditions,
vehicle-trips associated with the planned development, total traffic volume projections, and
recommendations on roadway improvements. Two future scenarios have been analyzed for this
report:

        Short Term Future ­ Time period for the completion of proposed development.

        Long Term Future ­ The year 2035 was used to assess traffic impacts of the
        development at buildout in the long-range future.

II.      EXISTING CONDITIONS
A.       Surrounding Land Use

To the north of the proposed development is Fox Ridge Middle School and Cherokee Trail High
School. There are primarily residential land uses to the west of the site with undeveloped land to
the east of the site (planned to eventually be developed into residential uses). Immediately
south of the proposed development is a Jehovah Witness assembly hall facility.

B.       Existing Conditions

The existing roads in the vicinity of the site include Arapahoe Road, Smoky Hill Road, Oak Hill
Way, and Powhaton Road. Arapahoe Road currently is a two-lane roadway in the vicinity of the
site, but its widening to four lanes (plus turn lanes at intersections) is planned for the near-term.
Smoky Hill Road is a five-lane roadway, with three westbound lanes and two eastbound lanes at
the intersection with Oak Hill Way (plus intersection turn lanes) and it narrows to a two-lane
roadway at its intersection with Powhaton Road. This roadway is currently not constructed
adjacent to the site, but it is a four-lane roadway extending north of Arapahoe Road and a two-
lane facility extending south of Smoky Hill Road. Oak Hill Way is a two-lane collector roadway.

Traffic Volumes

Existing traffic volumes in the vicinity of the site are presented on Figure 3. The AM and PM
turning movements were recorded at the following intersections in late August 2013:

         Arapahoe Road / Oak Hill Way
         Arapahoe Road / Powhaton Road
         Smoky Hill Road / Oak Hill Way
         Smoky Hill Road / Powhaton Road

School was in session for the middle school and high school located north of Arapahoe Road at
the time when these counts were completed. Smoky Hill Road serves over 800 vehicles per
hour (both directions combined) during the AM peak hour and over 1000 vehicles per hour
during the evening peak hour. Arapahoe Road traffic levels are much less being on the order of
475 vehicles per hour west of Oak Hill Drive during the AM peak hour and slightly over 200
vehicles per hour during the PM peak hour. The traffic levels along Arapahoe Road near Oak
Hill Way are heavily influenced by the Cherokee Trail High School and Fox Ridge Middle School
located along the north side of Arapahoe Road. As Figure 3 also shows, a notable number of
pedestrians are crossing Arapahoe Road, the timing of which corresponds to school operation
times. The west leg of the Arapahoe/Oak Hill intersection is heavy crossed by foot in the
morning before the first school bell, and the east leg is heavily crossed in the afternoon when
school lets out.

Traffic Operations

Traffic operations within the study area were evaluated according to techniques documented in
the Highway Capacity Manual, (Transportation Research Board, 2010) using the existing traffic
volumes and intersection geometry. Level of Service (LOS) is a qualitative measure of traffic
operational conditions, based on roadway capacity and vehicle delay. LOS is described by a
letter designation ranging from A to F, with LOS A representing almost free-flow travel, while
LOS F represents congested conditions. For signalized intersections, LOS is calculated for the
entire intersection while LOS for unsignalized intersections is calculated for movements which
must yield right-of-way to other traffic movements.

Figure 3 shows the results of the existing conditions analysis, including existing traffic control
and current intersection geometry. As shown, the yielding movements at the stop controlled
intersection operate at LOS C or better with the exception of the northeast approach at the
intersection of Smoky Hill Road and Glasgow Drive (Oak Hill Way) and the westbound approach
at Smoky Hill Road and Powhaton. The Glasgow Drive approach operates at LOS E during the
PM peak hour under stop-sign control due to the heavy through traffic along Smoky Hill Road.
The westbound approach at the Smoky Hill/Powhaton intersection also operates at LOS E, but
this occurs during the AM peak hour as a result of the heavy conflicting northbound left turn
movement and its lane-combination with the westbound left turn movement.

Figure 6 provides the short term background volumes and levels of service. As shown, the
yielding movements at the unsignalized intersections with Arapahoe Road would operate at
LOS C or better. The eastbound left-turning movement and westbound approach at the
intersection of Smoky Hill Road and Powhaton Road would operate at LOS E during the AM
peak hour. The northeast approach at the intersection of Smoky Hill Road and Oak Hill Road
will operate at LOS E during the PM peak hour and the westbound approach at Smoky
Hill/Powhaton will function at LOS E during the AM peak hour as occurs today. Additionally, the
eastbound left turn movement at Smoky/Powhaton will function at LOS E as well, but very few
drivers are anticipated to make this movement.

The nature of the traffic forecasts suggests that Powhaton Road should be free-flow at
Arapahoe Road with the east and west approaches being subject to side-street stop. The similar
configuration should be implemented at Smoky Hill/Powhaton with the eastbound right turn
movement continue to be a yield-controlled movement as exists today. However, the peak hour
traffic projections at these intersections are light and other acceptable traffic control
configurations are possible.

The peak hour traffic utilizing the intersection Arapahoe Road and Oak Hill Way is not
anticipated to drive the need for signalization. However, the nature of pedestrian use associated
with the high school and middle school might. The Manual on Uniform Traffic Control (MUTCD),
published by the Federal Highway Association, contains numerous technical warrants with
respect to signalization. Many are related to mainline and side street traffic demands, but others
are related to pedestrian activity as well. Specifically, Warrant Number 5 pertains to school
children crossing a busier roadway. This specific warrant is satisfied when too few gaps in
mainline traffic are available to walk across the roadway, and there are at least 20 students
attempting to cross the busy street within an hour. Based on the counts collected and given the
impending widening of Arapahoe Road to five lanes (requiring a larger gap in Arapahoe Road
traffic), there will not be enough adequate-sized gaps in Arapahoe Road traffic (including the
eastbound left turn movement into the school campus). Further, the number of students
crossing the road exceeds 20 per hour, and a close look at the raw data count show that most
of the pedestrian crossings occur within a 30-minute period in the morning and a 15-minute
period in the afternoon.

As such, signalization or some other traffic control treatment for students crossing Arapahoe
Road is warranted. Options are discussed in a later section of the report.

2035 Background Traffic

Long term background traffic volumes were determined based on the future traffic growth
projections in the Denver Regional Council of Governments (DRCOG) travel demand model and
from the Southeast Area Transportation Study (SEATS) traffic projections. Long term
background traffic volumes and levels of service are provided in Figure 7. Smoky Hill Road is
anticipated to serve approximate 2000 vehicles per hour during the AM peak hour and 2500
vehicles per hour during the PM peak hour; more than twice the demand that exists today.
Arapahoe Road will also increase to approximately 1000 and 800 vehicles per hour during the
AM and PM peak hours, respectively. These too represent a significant increase over existing
demands.

The intersection of Smoky Hill Road and Powhaton Road is anticipated to meet signal warrants
based on peak hour traffic, while the intersection of Smoky Hill Road and Oak Hill Way is
expected to be very close to warranting signalization given just background traffic demands.
The signalized intersection of Smoky Hill Road and Powhaton Road would operate at LOS B
during both peak hours with the 2035 background traffic. The intersection of Smoky Hill Road
and Oak Hill Way would operate at LOS A during both peak hours if signalized. If not signalized,
the northeast and southwest movements would all operate at LOS F during both peak hours.

The intersection of Arapahoe Road and Powhaton Road would not operate acceptably under
two-way stop control. Rather, all-way stop control would result in reasonable operations at this
intersection given 2035 background traffic.

IV.      TOTAL TRAFFIC CONDITIONS
A.       Short Term Total Traffic

The site generated traffic was added to the short term background traffic to obtain the short term
total traffic volumes shown in Figures 8. With respect to operations given these projections, the
turning movements at the stop controlled intersections along Arapahoe Road are all projected to
operate at LOS C during both peak hours given the same traffic control as analyzed with the
background traffic scenario. As mentioned, the intersection of Arapahoe Road and Oak Hill Way
traffic control warrants action today given the level of traffic traveling along Arapahoe road and
the magnitude of school children crossing Arapahoe Road. While a traffic signal would benefit
crossing school children, the traffic levels alone would not come close to meeting a signal
warrant. As such, signalization would be applicable to only short periods of time during the day.
Other options to fully signalizing the Arapahoe/Oak Hill intersection include:

         Flashing Warning Beacons. This option would assist in acquiring driver's attention to
         the existing cross-walk. However, this treatment is not always effective in conveying the
         need to stop when the cross-walk is in use. This option may have limited value.

         HAWK (High-intensity Activated crossWalK) Signal. This option entails placing
         specially designed signal heads aimed at east and west at traffic along Arapahoe Road.
         Upon pedestrians triggering a push button, flashing yellow, followed by a solid yellow,
         followed by a solid red light requiring Arapahoe Road drivers to stop. A flashing/toggling
         red light then takes place allowing drivers to cautiously proceed IF pedestrian is not in
         the crosswalk. Once the cycle is complete, the lights turn off and are blank until
         activated again.

         These signals are typically intended for mid-block locations, but installations have
         occurred at minor cross-street intersections. Issues with an installing this treatment at an
         intersection pertain to controlling the side-street approach traffic upon activation. With
         respect to the Arapahoe Road/Oak Hill Way intersection, the question would be which
         side of the intersection would this treatment be installed. The pedestrian count data
         indicate that the vast majority of the pedestrian cross the west leg of the intersection
         during the AM period and the east leg during the PM period. As such, this option may
         only address one of the peak periods since it would need to be placed on either the west
         side or east side of the intersection.

V.      SUMMARY AND RECOMMENDATIONS
The proposed development is located east of Oak Hill Way between Smoky Hill Road and
Arapahoe Road in Aurora, Colorado. The development includes 142 single family homes.
Access to the development will be via Oak Hill Road and an existing access onto Smoky Hill
Road.

Two future scenarios were analyzed for this report:

        Short Term Future ­ Time period for the completion of the proposed development. This
        scenario assumes that Arapahoe Road is widened to four lanes west of Powhaton Road
        and that Powhaton Road is constructed as a two-lane between Arapahoe Road and
        Smoky Hill Road.

        Long Term Future ­ The year 2035 was used to assess traffic impacts of the
        development in the long-range future.

The following is a summary of the findings and recommendations related to the analysis for the
development:

        At build out, this development will generate approximately 1,350 trips during the day with
        107 of these trips occurring during the AM peak and 142 trips occurring during the PM
        peak.

        The Oak Hill Way and Powhaton Road intersections along Smoky Hill Road are
        projected to meet signal warrants based on the peak hour volumes in the long term
        scenarios. These intersections would operate at LOS C or better when signalized. The
        three-quarter movement intersection between these two will also operate at acceptable
        LOS's (LOS B for each movement).

        The intersections on Arapahoe Road are not projected to meet warrants based on traffic
        demands alone. If left under stop-sign control, the turning movements at these
        intersections would operate at LOS D or better in the long term scenarios.

        The intersection of Arapahoe Road and Oak Hill Way also serves as the access to Fox
        Ridge Middle School located along the north side of Arapahoe Road. As such, there is a
        notable amount of school children crossing Arapahoe Road at this location, primarily
        northbound across the west leg during the morning peak and southbound across the
        east leg during the afternoon peak. Per the MUTCD School Signal Warrant criteria
        pertaining to gaps in traffic and the magnitude of school children crossing, a traffic signal
        is warranted today for these before-school and after-school times; the signal is not
        warranted from a traffic volume perspective. MUTCD also indicates that other measures
        should be considered. Options could include:




                                                                                             Page 18
Serenity Ridge                                                         Traffic Impact Analysis



          Flashing Warning beacons
          HAWK pedestrian signal
          Crossing guards
          All-Way stop control

      There are pros and cons to full signalization and to each of the options listed above.
      Additional discussion with City staff and possibly additional analyses would be
      appropriate to identify the best traffic control treatment at this intersection.
