Meeting Purpose

The purpose of the meeting was to identify problem areas on John Street in Matthews that are of
local concern and to solicit input on possible solutions.

Meeting Summary

Ms. Bereis explained briefly the purpose of the study and project goals. Ms. Bereis then asked
the Town of Matthews representatives to identify any areas along the existing roadway that stand
out as problem areas (hot spots) such as intersections within the Town limits.

   It is difficult to exit, particularly via a left turn, the Pine Forest subdivision (southwest side of
    John Street and accessed from Clearbrook Road). This is due to the major congestion on
    John Street, which is essentially from I-485 to Trade Street.

   Morningwood Drive, which provides access to the Eastwood Forest neighborhood, is used as
    cut-through route to get to McKee Road (via Pleasant Plains Road). As such, the intersection
    of Morningwood Drive with John Street is "much busier than it should be". This is likely to
    continue until the McKee road extension project is constructed.

   Traffic flow is restricted on John Street through Matthews due primarily to "pure traffic
    volumes" (rather than excessive driveways and traffic control) and the smaller cross section
    through Town. Many people try to avoid the major congestion at the Trade Street
    intersection by using Main Street as a cut through. There are `hot spots' at localized areas
    where the Town suggests improvements be considered.


                                                                                                        1
Meeting Summary
Town of Matthews  June 19, 2007

   One such area is the Petro gas station on the corner of John and Trade Streets. Currently, this
    business has five (5) driveways, two (2) on Trade Street and three (3) on John Street. The
    Town suggested that traffic flow near this intersection could be improved somewhat by
    closing/consolidating these driveways.

   The Town perceives the heavy truck traffic to impede traffic flow during peak periods. One
    potential solution is to restrict these heavy vehicles at certain times of the day.

   The Town suggests that signal timings be re-evaluated at the I-485 interchange ramps (the
    perception is that more time is allocated than needed for the left turn movement onto I-485 in
    the PM peak period).

   The Town also suggests evaluating the use of permissive left turn lanes in place of protected

   There is concern about the timing of improvements John Street relative to cross street

         o McKee Road extension from John Street to Campus Ridge Road (scheduled for

         o Grey Lock Ridge Road  this project is in the Thoroughfare Plan. Currently, John
           Street forms a T-intersection with this road. When it is built out, it will provide
           access into the proposed Sportsplex. In addition, there is a proposed subdivision on
           east of John Street and adjacent to/west of the proposed extension.

   The Town indicated that bicycle/pedestrian use along John Street should not be precluded
    with any proposed short term improvements.

Meeting Purpose

The purpose of the meeting was to identify problem areas on Old Monroe Road that are of
concern to the communities and to solicit input on possible solutions.

Meeting Summary

Ms. Bereis explained briefly the purpose of the Old Monroe Road study and project goals. Ms.
Bereis then asked Mr. Matthews if there are any areas along the existing roadway that stand out
as problem areas (hot spots) such as intersections within the Stallings area.

Mr. Matthews indicated that Old Monroe Road at Campus Ridge Road can be a "troublesome"
intersection (i.e. congested). Campus Ridge Road carries a lot of traffic to the CPCC campus in
Matthews. Mr. Matthews noted also that although Pleasant Plains Road carries less traffic than
Campus Ridge Road, its intersection with Old Monroe Road can also be "troublesome", likely
due to the fact that it's a skewed intersection.

When asked about potential concerns with regard to solutions, Mr. Matthews noted that the
Council has expressed their concerns with any improvements to Old Monroe Road, including:

   Access to adjacent subdivisions (Morning Side Meadows, Lakewood Knolls, Kerry Greens,
    Parkside Townhomes, and Cherokee Woods in Indian Trail that is accessed from Catawba
    Circle). Specifically, the Town believes that improvements should not prevent left turn lanes
    in/out of these subdivisions.

   Similar to the concern listed in the first bullet, the Town also has concerns regarding access
    for single family homes and businesses that front the roadway.

   Should improvements require any additional right-of-way, there is a concern for
    encroachment on adjacent properties (single family residences and small businesses).


                                                                                                     1
Meeting Summary
Town of Stallings  June 14, 2007



   There is concern about the timing of improvements to Old Monroe Road relative to the
    potential multi-laning of Stallings Road (TIP Project U-3825, which proposes to improve
    Stallings Road by adding additional travel lanes between Old Monroe Road and US 74;
    scheduled for construction in 2011). Since improvements to Stallings Road are scheduled
    prior to the widening of Old Monroe Road, the Town is concerned that traffic conditions at
    the intersection and along Old Monroe Road could worsen with a four-laned Stallings Road
    `bringing' traffic to a roadway that is still two-lanes.

When asked what else the Town would like to be considered when developing short term
improvements (i.e. potential solutions), Mr. Matthews indicated that some of the single family
frontage could be under pressure to transition to non-residential uses (small commercial or office
uses) in the future. In addition, there is some vacant property that will likely be built out with
commercial development in the future. Because of this, and because of the potential problem
with numerous driveways, the Town would welcome solutions that create shared access for
existing/future commercial development.
