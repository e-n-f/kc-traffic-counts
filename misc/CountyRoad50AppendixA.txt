Each gap study results in three data tables: gaps in the northbound direction, gaps in the southbound
direction, and combined gaps which are gaps occurring in the northbound and southbound directions
simultaneously.  The southbound gaps are pertinent to those waiting to turn right onto southbound
CSAH 50 while the combined gaps are pertinent to those waiting to turn left onto northbound CSAH 50.
The number of gaps, the number of combined gaps 8 seconds or greater and the minimum and
maximum number of vehicles accommodated by the combined gaps 8 seconds or greater can be found
belowinTable3.

Based on the AASHTO A Policy on Geometric Design of Highways and Streets (commonly called the
Green Book), vehicles turning left onto CSAH 50 to go northbound from one of the study intersections
will need a gap in the combined traffic of at least eight seconds to be able to make the turn. The total
numberofgapseightsecondslongorgreaterateachintersectionisshownintheattacheddatatable.

For vehicles in a queue turning left onto CSAH 50 from Jaguar Avenue, the AASHTO Green Book
recommendsanadditional2.5secondsofgaptimeforeachofthevehiclesfollowingtheleadingvehicle.
The minimum number of vehicles accommodated by the gaps of 8 seconds or greater is the number of
combined 8+ second gaps available. The maximum number of vehicles accommodated by the gaps of 8
seconds or greater is the number of queued vehicles able to make a left turn during each available gap
based on 8 seconds for the lead car and 2.5 seconds for each additional car. It is assumed that all the
gaps 29 seconds or greater are 30 seconds.  The minimum and maximum number of vehicles
accommodatedbythegapsisshowninTable3.
