1.0 Introduction

The proposed Plyer Road Site is located on the southeast corner of the intersection of Plyler Road
and Unionville-Indian Trail Road in Indian Trail, North Carolina. As currently planned, the
development will consist of 387 multifamily units. The site plan shows one (1) driveway on
Unionville Indian Trail Road and one (1) driveway on Plyler Road. Figure 1 shows the site plan.

DAVENPORT was retained to determine the potential traffic impacts of this development and to
identify transportation improvements that may be required to accommodate the impacts of both
background traffic and new development traffic. The following intersections were included in the
study:

   ·      US 74 at Corporate Boulevard / Allen Road
   ·      US 74 at Unionville Indian Trail Road
   ·      US 74 at Plyler Road
   ·      Plyler Road at Site Access 1 (Proposed)
   ·      Unionville Indian Trail Road at Plyler Road / Corporate Boulevard
   ·      Unionville Indian Trail Road at Site Access 2 (Proposed)
   ·      Indian Trail Road at Unionville Indian Trail Road

The above-mentioned intersections were analyzed for the following scenarios:

   ·      2013 Existing Conditions
   ·      2016 Future No Build Conditions
   ·      2016 Future Build Conditions

The expected build-out year for this project is 2016. The AM (7-9 am) and PM (4-6 pm) peaks were
studied.

This Transportation Impact Analysis (TIA) was carried out based on North Carolina Department of
Transportation (NCDOT) typical standards. Information regarding the property was provided by
the developer, Bonterra Builders.

3.0 Approved Developments and Committed Improvements
3.1    Approved Developments
Approved developments are developments that have been recently approved in the area, but not yet
constructed. The 3% annual traffic growth rate was considered adequate to account for future
traffic.
3.2    Committed Improvements
Committed Improvements are improvements that are planned by NCDOT, a local municipality, or a
developer in the area, but not yet constructed. Per NCDOT, there are no committed improvements
in the vicinity of the project.

5.0 Capacity Analysis

5.1    Level of Service Evaluation Criteria

The Transportation Research Board's Highway Capacity Manual (HCM) utilizes a term "level of
service" to measure how traffic operates in intersections and on roadway segments. There are
currently six levels of service ranging from A to F. Level of service "A" represents the best
conditions and Level of Service "F" represents the worst. Synchro Traffic Modeling software was
used to determine the level of service for studied intersections. Note for unsignalized intersection
analysis, the level of service noted is for the worst approach of the intersection. This is typically the
left turn movement for the side street approach, due to the number of opposing movements. All
worksheet reports from the analyses can be found in the Appendix.

5.2   Discussion of Results

The results of the study are discussed by intersection below:

US 74 at Corporate Boulevard / Allen Way

This unsignalized intersection has a "left-over" configuration to allow left in, right in, and right out
movement only. Currently, this intersection operates at LOS C during AM and PM peaks. In 2016
future no build conditions, LOS C is expected in AM and PM peaks. With the addition of proposed
site traffic, LOS D is expected in the AM peak and LOS C during the PM peak. Auxiliary left and
right turn lanes are currently in place at this intersection. No improvements are recommended.

US 74 at Unionville Indian Trail Road

This signalized intersection operates at LOS C during the existing AM and PM peaks. In 2016 future
no build conditions, LOS C is expected in AM and PM peaks. With the addition of future site
traffic, LOS C is expected in the AM peak and LOS D in the PM peak. No improvements are
recommended at this intersection.

US 74 at Plyler Road

This unsignalized, right in right out intersection operates at LOS A during existing AM and PM
peaks. In 2016 future no build conditions, LOS B is expected in AM and PM peaks. With the
addition of future site traffic, the level of service is expected to remain at LOS B in AM and PM
peaks. No improvements are recommended at this intersection.

Plyler Road at Site Access 1

In 2016 future build conditions, this proposed access is expected to operate at LOS A in the AM
peak and LOS B in the PM peak. The need for an auxiliary left turn lane was determined based on
the figure titled "Warrant for Left and Right Turn Lanes" on page 80 of the NCDOT "Policy on
Street and Driveway Access to North Carolina Highways". Based on the projected traffic volumes, a
right turn lane is warranted at this driveway. We recommend providing an eastbound right turn lane
on Plyler Road with 100 feet of storage and appropriate taper.

Unionville Indian Trail Road at Plyler Road / Corporate Boulevard

This unsignalized intersection operates at LOS B during the existing AM and PM peaks. In 2016
future no build conditions, LOS B is expected in the AM peak and LOS C in the PM peak. With the
addition of future site traffic, the level of service is expected to remain at LOS B in the AM peak and
LOS C in the PM peak. No improvements are recommended at this intersection.




10/7/13                    13-633 Plyler Road Site ­ Transportation Impact Analysis                  16
Unionville Indian Trail Road at Site Access 2

In 2016 future build conditions, this proposed access is expected to operate at LOS B in the AM
peak and LOS B in the PM peak. The need for an auxiliary left turn lane was determined based on
the figure titled "Warrant for Left and Right Turn Lanes" on page 80 of the NCDOT "Policy on
Street and Driveway Access to North Carolina Highways". Based on the projected traffic volumes, a
right turn lane is warranted at this driveway. We recommend providing an eastbound right turn lane
on Unionville Indian Trail Road with 100 feet of storage and appropriate taper.

Indian Trail Road at Matthews Indian Trail Road

This signalized intersection operates at LOS C during existing AM and PM peaks. In 2016 future no
build conditions, LOS C is expected in the AM peak and LOS D in the PM peak. However, the
traffic simulation using Sim Traffic software indicates there are queuing issues at this intersection.
These queuing issues are mainly due to lack of capacity for left and right turn movements, since this
intersection has single lane approaches on all four legs. This is a background capacity issue which
exists regardless of the proposed development. The following improvements are warranted due
to queuing in background traffic conditions, and not as a result of the proposed site:

   ·      Provide left turn lane on both northbound and southbound directions of Indian Trail Road
          with 100 feet of storage and appropriate taper
   ·      Provide northbound right turn lane on Indian Trail Road with 200 feet of storage and
          appropriate taper
   ·      Provide westbound through/right combo lane on Matthews Indian Trail Road with 200 feet
          of storage and appropriate taper. Utilize existing pavement as full-length left turn lane
   ·      Provide eastbound left turn lane on Matthews Indian Trail Road with 100 feet of storage and
          appropriate taper

With the addition of future site traffic, LOS D is expected in AM and PM peaks. The proposed
development is anticipated to add only 2.4% to the traffic at this intersection in the AM peak hour
(39 vehicles) and 2.3% in the PM peak hour (46 vehicles). The background improvements noted
above will be adequate at this intersection.

6.0 Summary and Conclusion

The proposed Plyler Road Site is located on the southeast corner of the intersection of Plyler Road
and Unionville-Indian Trail Road in Indian Trail, North Carolina. As currently planned, the
development will consist of 387 multifamily units. The site plan shows one (1) driveway on
Unionville Indian Trail Road and one (1) driveway on Plyler Road. Figure 1 shows the site plan.

DAVENPORT was retained to determine the potential traffic impacts of this development and the
transportation improvements that may be required to accommodate these impacts. Based on trip
generation equations published in Trip Generation (Institute of Transportation Engineers, 8th
Edition, 2008), the proposed development has a trip generation potential of 194 AM peak trips and
231 PM peak trips.

Based on projected traffic volumes, a right turn lane is warranted at the proposed Site Access 1 on
Plyler Road. We also recommend a left turn lane at Site Access 2 on Unionville Indian Trail Road.
Additionally, we have identified the need for background improvements at the signalized
intersection of Indian Trail Road and Matthews Indian Trail Road. There are queuing issues at this
intersection, which are mainly due to lack of capacity for left and right turn movements, since the
intersection has single lane approaches on all four legs. Hence we have recommended auxiliary turn
lanes at this intersection due to background traffic, and not as a result of the proposed development.
The recommended improvements are illustrated in Figure 9.

In conclusion, this study has reviewed the impacts of both background traffic and this development
traffic, and has determined that with the recommended improvements in place, there will be
adequate capacity to accommodate future traffic. We recommend that the site driveways should be
constructed to comply with NCDOT design standards where applicable.
