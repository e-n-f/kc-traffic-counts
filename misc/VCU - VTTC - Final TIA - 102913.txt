1. INTRODUCTION
Ramey Kemp & Associates, Inc. (RKA) has performed this Traffic Impact Analysis (TIA) for
the proposed Virginia Treatment Center for Children (VTCC) located in the northwest
quadrant of US 1 (Brook Road) at Sherwood Avenue in Richmond, Virginia. The purpose of
this study is to determine the potential impacts on the surrounding transportation system
created by traffic generated by the proposed facility, and recommend improvements needed to
mitigate the impacts. The study analyzes existing (2013), no-build (2015), and build (2015)
traffic conditions during the weekday AM and PM peak hours.


1.1. Site Location and Study Area
The proposed 32-bed facility will be located on the north side of Sherwood Avenue between I-
95 and the existing hospital. Figure 1 shows the site location map. The scope of this study
was developed based on discussion with the City of Richmond and is outlined in the
Memorandum of Understanding (MOU), which is in the Appendix.


The following intersections are included in the study area:

       Brook Road at Brookland Park Boulevard
       Brook Road at Sherwood Avenue
       Brook Road at Site Driveway
       Sherwood Avenue at Main Driveway
       Sherwood Avenue at Service Driveway


1.2. Existing Land Uses
The property is currently vacant, and is zoned R-3. The surrounding land uses are industrial to
the west, institutional to the south and east, and residential to the north.

2. TRAFFIC ANALYSIS PROCEDURE
The traffic capacity analysis was performed with Synchro 8, which is a comprehensive
software package that allows the user to determine the level-of-service (LOS) for the study
intersections based on the control delay thresholds specified in the 2010 Highway Capacity
Manual (HCM). LOS is a qualitative measure of traffic operation that ranges from LOS A,
which represents free-flow conditions, to LOS F, which represents congestion and long delays.


For signalized intersections, Synchro calculates the average control delay and queue length for
each lane group, and the LOS for the overall intersection. For unsignalized intersections,
Synchro calculates the average control delay and queue length for stop-controlled movements,
but does not provide an overall LOS for the intersection. Table 1 shows the control delay
thresholds for unsignalized and signalized movements.
                                      Table 1
                 Highway Capacity Manual ­ Levels-of-Service and Delay
   UNSIGNALIZED INTERSECTION                       SIGNALIZED INTERSECTION
        LEVEL            CONTROL DELAY               LEVEL             CONTROL DELAY
          OF              PER VEHICLE                  OF               PER VEHICLE
       SERVICE             (SECONDS)                SERVICE              (SECONDS)
          A                    0-10                    A                     0-10
          B                   10-15                    B                    10-20
          C                   15-25                    C                    20-35
          D                   25-35                    D                    35-55
          E                   35-50                    E                    55-80
           F                   >50                     F                     >80


LOS D is typically an acceptable overall LOS for signalized intersections, and it is common
for left-turn and minor street movements to experience LOS E or F at both signalized and
unsignalized intersections.

4. NO-BUILD (2015) TRAFFIC CONDITIONS
In order to account for growth of traffic and subsequent traffic conditions at a future year,
background traffic projections are needed. Background traffic is the component of traffic due
to the growth of the community and surrounding area that is anticipated to occur regardless of
whether or not the proposed development is constructed.


4.1. Background Traffic Growth
The following is a summary of the average daily traffic (ADT) volume data published by
VDOT for Brook Road in the vicinity of the site:

       2012 ­ 11,000 vehicles per day
       2009 ­ 11,000 vehicles per day

To be conservative, an annual growth rate of 3% was applied to the peak hour traffic volumes
at the study intersections. Figure 4 shows the no-build 2015 AM and PM peak hours traffic
volumes.


4.2. Approved Adjacent Development Traffic
Based on discussion with the City of Richmond, there are no approved developments that will
have an impact on the study area.


4.3. Analysis of No-Build (2015) Peak Hour Traffic Conditions
No-build (2015) peak hour traffic volumes were analyzed using the existing lane
configurations and traffic control conditions. The results of the analysis are presented in
Section 8 of this report.
