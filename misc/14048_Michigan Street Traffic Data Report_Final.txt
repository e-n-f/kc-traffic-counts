1.0 Introduction
This report summarizes the traffic data that was collected for the Michigan Street Corridor Study and
the Monroe Geometric and Traffic Operations Study. The data was collected for use with future studies
as directed by the City. The data summarized in this report is from two sources:

     ·    2010 traffic data collected by the Study team as part of this study
     ·    Historic traffic data compiled from other studies, as provided by the City

The intent of this data collection effort is to:

     ·     capture all available traffic data that can be used in the future for the anticipated Michigan
           Street Corridor analysis and other studies; and
     ·     collect accurate traffic data for the Coldbrook/Monroe/Ottawa study, whose report is being
           completed under a separate cover.

In total the traffic data for 19 intersections are summarized in this report, 8 provided by the City and 11
counted by TEA, Inc. In addition TEA collected 24 hour traffic data by placing tube counters at 12
locations.

2.0 2010 Traffic Count Data Collection
TEA Inc. collected turning volume traffic counts at 11 locations and tube counters at 12 locations within
the potential Michigan Street study area and at the Coldbrook/Monroe/Ottawa intersection, to
supplement traffic data provided by the City and to provide new data for the
Coldbrook/Monroe/Ottawa Intersection Study. Figure 2.0 shows the tube and intersection locations
within the study corridor.

3.0 Additional Data Sources

The City of Grand Rapids provided draft copies of the Grand Rapids Metropolitan Area Signal System
Optimization Project Phase I and II Reports. The Phase II report provided complete traffic data for
future studies of the Michigan Street Corridor. The reports summarize the data collection plan for Phase
I and II of the Grand Rapids Area Traffic Signal Timing Optimization Project, and are summarized in this
document.

As part of the Phase I Signal System Optimization Project, a total of 84 intersections in Grand Rapids and
the surrounding communities were studied and evaluated for improved safety and traffic flow. Those
intersections are located along four major arterials including Fulton Street, Division Avenue, 44th Street,
and Alpine Avenue.

The traffic data included within Phase I and II as summarized in this Data Collection document, include
three types of counts:

    1. Peak hour turning movments counts at intersections, 7:00 ­ 9:00AM, 11:00AM ­ 1:00PM and
       4:00 ­ 6:00PM
    2. 24 hour coverage (machine) counts along mid-block roadway segments (during typical
       weekdays)
    3. 24 hour intersection appraoch counts (during off peak/weekends)

Only intersections displayed in Figure 2 are summarized as part of this Traffic Data Collection report.
