I.     INTRODUCTION
Chambers High Point is a proposed mixed-use development to be located within the Town of
Parker, Colorado. The approximate 31 acre site is located along the south side of E-470 just
west of Chambers Road. Figure 1 illustrates the location of the site and the adjacent primary
roadway network.

The current site plan concept for the Chambers High Point development is depicted on Figure
2. As indicated, a Frontage Road would bisect the site parallel to E-470, providing site access to
Chambers Road. In the future, it is envisioned that the Frontage Road would extended west to
Peoria Street, when lands to the west of the Chambers High Point site are developed. Proposed
site land uses include Highway Commercial uses along the north side of the Frontage Road,
and a mix of Multi-Family Residential and Commercial uses in the southern portion of the site.
These site land uses would access the Frontage Road via three full-movement intersections. In
addition, a Right-IN/Right-Out (RIRO) access is proposed along Chambers Road approximately
midway between E-470 and the Frontage Road.

The purpose of this Traffic Impact Analysis is to evaluate the potential traffic impacts specific to
Chambers High Point and to identify any roadway or traffic control improvements required as a
result of this proposed development. The analyses consider two future scenarios:

       ·   Short Range Future. This scenario examines the traffic impacts on completion of the
           project in the near-term future (year 2015).

       ·   Long Range Future. This scenario examines the traffic impacts within the context of
           a year 2035 horizon.

II.    EXISTING CONDITIONS
A.     Land Use

The Chambers High Point site is currently vacant land. Immediately south of the site are large-
lot residential uses in Grandview Estates. The Stonegate development, partially developed with
residential uses (future uses within Stonegate include commercial development). Lands to the
north of E-470 are partially developed with commercial uses.

B.     Roadway System

E-470 forms the northern boundary of the site. In the vicinity of the site, this six-lane toll road
interchanges with Peoria Street, Chambers Road, and Jordan Road. The posted speed limit is
75 miles per hour (MPH). Chambers Road forms the eastern site boundary. This north-south
arterial roadway consists of a basic four-lane divided cross section with a posted speed limit of
45 MPH. Aventerra Parkway, a four-lane major collector (posted 35 MPH), intersects Chambers
Road from the east, serving the Stonegate neighborhood. All intersections within the study area
are currently unsignalized.

C.     Traffic Volumes and Operations

AM and PM peak hour turning movement counts were recently conducted at the unsignalized
intersections along Chambers Road at the E-470 Ramps and Aventerra Parkway. (count data
sheets are included in Appendix A). Daily traffic counts for study area roadways were
estimated by applying a factor of 10 to the PM peak hour counts. Figure 3 illustrates the
existing traffic volumes within the study area. It can be seen that Chambers Road currently
experiences approximately 11,000 to 13,400 vehicles per day (VPD). Aventerra Parkway
experiences an estimated 4,500 VPD within the study area.

The existing peak hour volumes were used as the basis for intersection capacity analyses, the
results of which are also summarized on Figure 3 (analysis worksheets are included in
Appendix B). Level of Service (LOS) is a qualitative measure of traffic operational conditions,
based on roadway capacity and motorist delay. The 2000 HIGHWAY CAPACITY MANUAL
defines six levels of service, ranging from A to F, with LOS A representing the best possible
operating conditions and LOS F representing over-capacity, or congested conditions. In
urbanized areas, LOS D is considered to be acceptable for peak hour traffic operations.

As indicated, unsignalized traffic movements at the study area intersections currently operate
acceptably, at LOS C or better during the AM peak hour. During the PM peak hour, however,
some STOP sign controlled movements (primarily left-turns) experience congestion and delays.
This is due to the relatively high through-volumes along Chambers during the afternoon
commute; the existing traffic volumes suggest that traffic signals are not currently warranted at
these intersections.

III.     FUTURE CONDITIONS
A.       Site Trip Generation

As previously discussed, the proposed development at Chambers High Point would consist of a
mix of Highway Commercial (retail), Commercial (office), and Multi-Family Residential uses.
Based on the site plan concept and on density information from the developer, approximately
435,600 square feet of retail, 209,000 square feet of office, and 420 residential dwelling units
would be accommodated on-site.

The trip generation potential of the proposed land uses was conducted based on rates
contained in TRIP GENERATION, 8th Edition, Institute of Transportation Engineers, 2008.
Because some interaction between site land uses would be anticipated, trip reductions were
applied for Mixed Use Developments, per methodologies documented in TRIP GENERATION
HANDBOOK, Institute of Transportation Engineers, 1998 (Appendix C documents the mixed-
use analysis) . Table 1 summarizes the results.

B.     Trip Distribution And Traffic Assignment

The site trip generation estimates were distributed to the adjacent roadways based on existing
and projected future travel patterns. Figure 4 illustrates the estimated distribution for both Short
Range and Long Range Future scenarios. As shown, the future extension of the Frontage Road
west to Peoria Street is expected to reduce the site generated component of traffic volumes on
E-470 west of Chambers Road by the year 2035.

The resultant Short Range Future site generated traffic assignment is illustrated on Figure 5. As
shown, Chambers High Point is projected to contribute daily traffic volumes in the approximate
range of 4,090 to 12,270,050 VPD to Chambers Road. E-470 would experience site generated
volumes of about 4,090 VPD east and west of Chambers Road. Aventerra Parkway would
experience about 2,050 VPD.

The Long Range Future assignment is depicted on Figure 6. As shown, Chambers High Point
is projected to contribute daily traffic volumes in the approximate range of 4,090 to 9,200 VPD to
Chambers Road. The reduction from the Short Range Future is due to site traffic using the
Frontage Road, which is projected to be extended west of the site to Peoria Street.
Consequently, E-470 would experience site generated volumes of about 1,020 VPD west of
Chambers Road; east of Chambers Road, the site contribution would remain at 4,090 VPD.
Aventerra Parkway would continue to experience about 2,050 VPD in Chambers High Point
generated traffic.

C.     Background Traffic

Short Range Future

Background traffic is the component of roadway volumes which would utilize the adjacent
roadway system regardless of site development. The Denver Regional Council of Governments
(DRCOG) 2035 traffic model indicates that area traffic volumes are projected to grow at a rate of
5.1 percent per year over the existing volumes. Therefore, this rate was applied to obtain year
2015 background traffic volumes for the Short Range Future scenario, as shown on Figure 7.
Because the Frontage Road connection is seen as a longer term improvement, background
traffic volumes are not applicable in this scenario. It can be seen that daily traffic along
Chambers Road would be in the approximate range of 13,800 to 16,400 VPD within the study
area. Aventerra Parkway would experience about 5,500 VPD in background traffic.

The Short Range Future background traffic volumes were used as the basis for intersection
capacity analyses, the results of which are also shown on Figure 7 (LOS worksheets are
included in Appendix D). As indicated, unsignalized traffic operations along Chambers Road
would be generally acceptable, with the exception of the PM Peak hour side-street movements,
which would continue to experience congestion and delays. At the Chambers Road/E-470
westbound ramp intersection, side-street movements would also experience delays during the
AM peak hour. The projected volumes at this intersection approach peak hour criteria to warrant
a traffic signal, per the MANUAL ON UNIFORM TRAFFIC CONTROL DEVICES, FHWA, 2009
(MUTCD).

Long Range Future

The Long Range Future (year 2035) background traffic volumes, shown on Figure 8, are also
based on the 5.1 percent annual growth trend extracted from the DRCOG model. Although not
included in the model network, the Frontage Road is envisioned to be completed within this time
frame in conjunction with potential development to the west of the Chambers High Point site.
Absent specific development proposals for these lands, it has been estimated that
approximately 7,500 VPD in background traffic would use the Frontage Road through the site.
Daily traffic along Chambers Road would increase to approximately 30,500 to 36,400 VPD
within the study area. Aventerra Parkway would experience about 5,500 VPD in background
traffic.

The Long Range Future background traffic volumes were used as the basis for intersection
capacity analyses, the results of which are also shown on Figure 8 (LOS worksheets are
included in Appendix D). As depicted, traffic signals are anticipated within this time frame at the
intersections along Chambers Road. Signalized operations would be acceptable, at LOS D or
better, during peak times. Because dual left-turn lanes are typically considered when the
associated volume exceeds 300 vehicles per hour (VPH), background geometric improvements
would include dual left turn lanes northbound at the E-470 westbound ramps, and southbound
at Aventerra Parkway.

D.     Total Traffic Conditions

Short Range Future

The Short Range Future site generated traffic volumes (Figure 5) were added to the
corresponding background volumes (Figure 7) to produce the Short Range Future total traffic
volumes shown on Figure 9. As indicated, total daily traffic along Chambers Road would
increase to approximately 28,600 VPD adjacent to the site. The Frontage Road would
experience about 20,810 VPD, consistent with a basic four-lane cross section. Aventerra
Parkway would experience about 7,550 VPD in the year 2015.

The Short Range Future total peak hour volumes were used as the basis for intersection
capacity analyses, the results of which are also summarized on Figure 7 (analysis worksheets
are included in Appendix E). By the year 2015, traffic signals would be anticipated along
Chambers Road at the E-470 westbound ramp, Frontage Road, and Aventerra Parkway

E.     Signal Progression Analysis

The ability to travel along a signalized corridor without stopping for red signal indications is
termed progressive movement. Arterial roadways with high levels of progression experience
reduced delays and fewer stops. Signal progression is a function of signal spacing, signal
timing, and travel speed. A key measure of progression is efficiency: the percent of the system
cycle length available for progressive movement. Typically, an efficiency of 37 percent or higher
is considered excellent, 25 to 36 percent is good, 13 to 24 percent is fair, and efficiencies of less
than 13 percent indicate poor levels of progression.

A progression analysis was conducted using the SYNCHRO traffic analysis software for
Chambers Road within the study area. The progression analysis utilized Long Range Future AM
and PM peak hour traffic volume projections, system cycle lengths of 100 seconds (AM peak
hour and 110 seconds (PM peak hour), and a progression speed of 45 MPH. The resultant time-
space diagrams are included in Appendix F. It was determined that progression during the AM
peak hour would have an efficiency of 29.5 percent, indicating good progression. The PM peak
hour efficiency would be at 26.8 percent, also good.

IV.       SUMMARY AND RECOMMENDATIONS
Chambers High Point is a proposed 31 acre mixed-use development to be located along the
south side of E-470 just west of Chambers Road. The development concept includes
approximately 435,600 square feet of retail, 209,000 square feet of office, and 420 residential
dwelling units. The site would be bisected by a Frontage Road that would provide primary site
via three full-movement intersections. In addition, a Right-IN/Right-Out (RIRO) access is
proposed along Chambers Road approximately midway between E-470 and the Frontage Road.

The proposed land uses at Chambers High Point would have the potential to generate
approximately 20,460 vehicle trips on a daily basis. About 975 of these trips would occur in the
AM peak hour, and about 1,910 of these trips would occur in the PM peak hour. The potential
traffic impacts due to this additional traffic were evaluated under both Short Range Future (year
2015) and Long Range Future (year 2035) scenarios. In general, the adjacent roadway system
would have sufficient capacity to accommodate the projected traffic volumes, subject to the
following improvement recommendations:

Short Range Future

      ·   Periodically monitor traffic volumes at the study area intersections and install traffic
          signals, as warranted. It is projected that the E-470 westbound ramps, Frontage Road,
          and Aventerra Parkway intersections along Chambers Road would require signalization
          within the Short Range Future.

      ·   Install dual left-turn lanes on northbound Chambers Road at the Frontage Road
          intersection.

      ·   Install dual left-turn lanes on southbound Chambers Road at the Aventerra Parkway
          intersection.

      ·   Install right-turn auxiliary laneage along southbound Chambers Road to serve the
          proposed RIRO access and the Frontage Road.

Long Range Future

      ·   Periodically monitor traffic volumes at the study area intersections and install traffic
          signals, as warranted. It is projected that the Chambers Road/E-470 eastbound ramp
          intersection and the two easternmost site accesses along the Frontage Road would
          require signalization in the Long Range Future.

      ·   Widen Chambers Road to six through-lanes between E-470 and The Frontage Road.
          This section of Chambers Road is projected to experience approximately 45,600 VPD by
          the year 2035.
