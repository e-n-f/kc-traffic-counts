                 Vanasse Hangen Brustlin, Inc. (VHB) has prepared this memorandum to provide responses to the
                 traffic comments submitted in the Fay, Spofford, Thorndike (FST) comment letter on the Proposed
                 Outpatient Care Facility at 620 Washington Street Traffic Impact and Access Study (TIAS), dated
                 November 9, 2007 and traffic comments from the town staff. FST has previously reviewed the
                 project during the Town of Winchester's Town Meeting review process, after which the Town of
                 Winchester approved the site for re-zoning. FST's comments provided during the re-zoning process
                 were addressed in the November 2007 TIAS.


                 Additionally, the Proponent's development team met with FST engineers and town staff on
                 November 15, 2007 to discuss VHB's preliminary responses to traffic related comments. The
                 responses contained in this memorandum pertain to the outstanding issues identified by FST and
                 town staff. This memorandum paraphrases each comment, in italics, and provides responses in a
                 format consistent with that used in the FST comment letter.


                 RESPONSE TO OUTSTANDING COMMENTS

                 Comment 1: An evaluation of the intersections of Forest Street at Cross Street and Forest Street at Highland
                 Avenue should be provided.


                 Response 1: The intersections included in the TIAS study area were originally identified with the
                 input of the Winchester Engineering Department. Based on this input, all critical intersections
                 identified in proximity to the site were included in the TIAS. The adequacy of the study area was
                 confirmed by FST in their comment letter dated April 19, 2007 (submitted during the project's re-
                 zoning application review), which states that "the VHB Study area adequately includes all major
                 intersection[s] that will be affected." However, based on continued requests from the town staff, an
                 evaluation of the projected operations at the two requested additional intersections has been
                 conducted.

    Comment 3: During the Development Agreement review process, it was indicated that the site driveway
    garage exit is located in an awkward location for existing traffic.


    Response 3: As discussed during the November 15, 2007 meeting with town staff, the Proponent will
    investigate the provision of an electronic gate to prohibit vehicles within the garage from exiting via
    this driveway during critical periods (likely weekday afternoons), along with appropriate internal
    garage signage.


    Comment 4: A discussion of the relative benefits between the two potential improvement plans for the Cross
    Street/Holton Street/East Street/Lowell Avenue intersection should be provided.


    Response 4: Two potential improvement concepts for the Cross Street/Holton Street/East
    Street/Lowell Avenue intersection were included in the TIAS. As suggested by the Town of
    Winchester's traffic peer review consultant during the re-zoning application review process for this
    project, the concepts provide two different options for restricting vehicular access to East Street, with
    the intention of reducing cut-through traffic along East Street:


         ·     Concept 1: a localized one-way eastbound travel restriction on East Street at Holton Street
               would prohibit all westbound traffic from accessing East Street at this location.
         ·     Concept 2: access to East Street at this intersection would be eliminated through construction
               of a dead-end on East Street, with provision for emergency vehicle access only.


    The primary difference between the two proposed concepts is the proposal to only partially restrict
    vehicular access to/from East Street under Concept 1 vs. entirely prohibiting vehicular access under
    Concept 2. Therefore, while both concepts would be expected to result in some diversion of
    commuter vehicles to other residential streets (likely to the west of the Cross Street/Holton
    Street/East Street/Lowell Street intersection) the Concept 2 design would be expected to result in a
    greater magnitude of diversions. Emergency vehicle access to East Street could be maintained under
    the Concept 2 design through the use of a breakaway gate for emergency vehicles only.


    Additionally, based on the greater restriction of access to and from East Street, the Concept 2 design
    would likely result in a reduction in overall turning movement activity at the Cross Street/Holton
    Street/East Street/Lowell Street intersection. Therefore, the Concept 2 design is likely to result in
    slightly improved overall operations at the intersection, as compared to the Concept 1 design.


    It should be noted that any improvement measures requested by the neighborhood residents will
    ultimately be subject to the Town of Winchester's review processes and approval by the Winchester
    Board of Selectmen.


    Comment 5: A discussion of the relative benefits of the proposed potential traffic-calming improvements in the
    Brookside Avenue and Sunset Road neighborhoods should be provided. It should be noted that Winchester
    does not currently have a `residential parking' program.


    Response 5: The Proponent met with the residents of the Brookside Avenue and Sunset Road
    neighborhoods to discuss potential traffic-calming measures to reduce speeds and cut-through
    traffic in these neighborhoods as presented in the TIAS. While the issues encountered by each
    neighborhood differ somewhat, the residents have generally expressed the following priorities and
    concerns:

    proposed bikeway, which is located along the southern site frontage, will terminate at Washington

    City of Woburn. The Proponent intends to continue working with the Town of Winchester to
    integrate an appropriate accommodation for bicyclists and pedestrians using the Tri-Community
    Bikeway/Greenway into the off-site design plans.
