                                           EXECUTIVE SUMMARY 


McMahon has completed a transportation analysis for the redevelopment of a parcel of land located on 

the  north  side  of  Royal  Palm  Boulevard  just  east  of  Riverside  Drive,  in  the  City  of  Margate.   The  site 

currently  includes  the  First  Presbyterian  Church.    The  proposed  development,  with  an  anticipated 

buildout year of 2016, will include 125 residential townhomes. 

 

Study Area 

The study area included the following intersections: 

         Royal Palm Boulevard at Riverside Drive 
         Royal Palm Boulevard at Sunflower Drive 
         Royal Palm Boulevard at Rock Island Road 
 

Data Collection  

Existing  (2013)  turning  movement  counts  were  collected  at  the  study  intersections  between  Tuesday, 

February 5, 2013 and Thursday, February 7, 2013 from 7:00 AM to 9:00 AM and from 4:00 PM to 6:00 PM.  

Vehicular queues along Royal Palm Boulevard and along Sunflower Drive were also observed.     

 

Trip Generation/Distribution/Access 

Trips  generated  by  the  existing  and  proposed  developments  were  estimated  based  on  trip  generation 

rates and equations published in the Institute of Transportation Engineers (ITE), Trip Generation Manual, 

9th Edition.  Access to the site is being proposed via one (1) existing fullaccess driveway connection along 

Royal Palm Boulevard, located on the east end of the site.  The project trip distribution for the proposed 

development  was  based  on  a  review  of  existing  travel  patterns  surrounding  the  project  site  and  the 

proximity to major thoroughfare roadways in the vicinity of the project site.  Based on a review of these 

volumes, the following distribution is proposed:  40 percent to/from the west along Royal Palm Boulevard 

and 60 percent to/from the east along Royal Palm Boulevard. 

 

Intersection Level of Service Analysis 

The capacity analysis at the study intersections was performed with no trip credit taken for the existing 

church  on  the  site.   The  site  was  assumed  to  be  vacant  for  analysis  purpose  to  provide  a  conservative 
analysis.   The  intersection  of  Royal  Palm  Boulevard  and  Riverside  Drive  currently  operates  at  LOS  D 

during  the  AM  peak  hour  and  at  LOS  E  during  the  PM  peak  hour.    At  the  project  buildout,  the 

intersection is expected to continue to operate at LOS D during the AM peak hour and LOS E during the 

PM  peak  hour,  with  minimal  vehicular  delay  increases  of  1.7  seconds  per  vehicle  and  1.1  seconds  per 

vehicle, respectively.  The intersection of Royal Palm Boulevard at Rock Island Road currently operates at 

LOS  E  during  the  AM  and  PM  peak  hours.    At  the  project  buildout,  the  intersection  is  expected  to 

continue to operate at LOS E during the AM and PM peak hours, with minimal vehicular delay increases 

of 3.7 seconds per vehicle and 4.8 seconds per vehicle, respectively.  At the intersection of Royal Palm 

Boulevard/Sunflower  Drive,  traffic  along  Royal  Palm  Boulevard  currently  operates  at  LOS  A.   At  the 

project buildout, Royal Palm Boulevard is anticipated to continue to operate at LOS A, with no vehicular 

delay increase during the AM peak hour and with a minimal vehicular delay of 0.2 seconds per vehicle 

during the PM peak hour.  Based on field observations, a typical vehicular queue of two (2) vehicles or 

less is anticipated for the northbound and southbound travel movements from Sunflower Drive to Royal 

Palm Boulevard.  Sufficient gaps are expected in the traffic stream along Royal Palm Boulevard to allow 

vehicles to complete their desired maneuver with minimal delay. 

 

Roadway Significance Analysis 

Broward County guidelines indicate a significance threshold for roadways of three (3) percent.  Project 

trips from the proposed development are expected to have a roadway impact of 1.27 percent on Royal 

Palm  Boulevard.    This  equates  to  only  43  percent  of  the  three  (3)  percent  significance  threshold  per 

Broward  County  guidelines.    Therefore,  the  proposed  development  is  expected  to  have 

minimal/insignificant impact on the surrounding roadway network.   

 

Gate Queuing Analysis 

The queue analysis indicates that sufficient stacking distance is being provided for visitor and resident 

access  to  avoid  any  potential  queue  backup  onto  Royal  Palm  Boulevard.   The  maximum  visitor  and 

resident queues are expected to be two (2) vehicles in each lane, including the vehicle being serviced.  The 

visitor lane will provide storage for more than twice the expected queue.  The resident lane will provide 

storage for more than three times the expected queue.

2.1     Roadway Characteristics 

Field reviews were conducted to obtain relevant roadway characteristics, including roadway geometries, 

speed  limit  information,  signal  operations,  turn  lane  restrictions  and  vehicular  queues  for  the  study 

intersections.  The existing lane geometry for the study intersections is graphically shown on Figure 2.  

Based on coordination with City staff, the intersections analyzed as part of this study were the following: 

 

        1. Royal Palm Boulevard at Riverside Drive:  This intersection is signalized. 

        2. Royal Palm Boulevard at Sunflower Drive:  This intersection is stop controlled. 

        3. Royal Palm Boulevard at Rock Island Road:  This intersection is signalized. 

 

2.2     Data Collection ­ Turning Movement Counts 

Existing  (2013)  turning  movement  counts  were  collected  at  the  study  intersections  between  Tuesday, 

February 5, 2013 and Thursday, February 7, 2013 from 7:00 AM to 9:00 AM and from 4:00 PM to 6:00 PM.  

The traffic volume data is included in Appendix B.  From the collected data, the AM and PM peak hours 

for the signalized intersections occur from 7:15 AM to 8:15 AM and from 5:00 PM to 6:00 PM, respectively. 

The AM and PM peak hours for the stop controlled intersection occur from 7:00 AM to 8:00 AM and from 

5:00 PM to 6:00 PM, respectively. 

 

2.3     Data Collection ­ Vehicular Queues 

Existing traffic queues were observed, during morning and afternoon peak periods, for vehicles travelling 

eastbound and westbound along Royal Palm Boulevard between Riverside Drive and Rock Island Road, 

as  well  as  vehicles  travelling  on  Sunflower  Drive  at  the  intersection  with  Royal  Palm  Boulevard.   No 

vehicular queues were observed along Royal Palm Boulevard in either the eastbound or westbound travel 

directions that impacted the intersection at Sunflower Drive.  On Sunflower Drive, the typical vehicular 

queue approaching Royal Palm Boulevard was determined to be two (2) vehicles during both the AM and 

PM peak hours.  A maximum queue of five (5) vehicles was observed twice during the AM peak hour; 

however, sufficient gaps were observed in the existing traffic stream along Royal Palm Boulevard to allow 

those vehicles to complete their desired maneuver with minimal delay.  The typical westbound leftturn 

vehicular queue onto Sunflower Drive was determined to be two (2) vehicles during both peak periods.

2.5     Intersection Capacity Analysis ­ Existing Traffic Conditions 

Intersection  capacity  analysis  was  performed  for  AM  and  PM  peak  hour  conditions  for  the  study 

intersections.  The analysis was performed using the Highway Capacity Manual methodology from the 

Synchro software.  Existing peak hour factors and truck factors were based on the collected data.  Where 

truck factors were below the default standard of two (2) percent, two (2) percent was utilized for analysis 

purpose.   The  existing  signal  timing  sheets  were  obtained  from  Broward  County  and  are  included  in 

Appendix D.  The intersection capacity analysis worksheets are included in Appendix D.   

 

Results of the analysis, summarized in Table 1, indicate that the intersection of Royal Palm Boulevard 

and Riverside Drive currently operates at Level of Service (LOS) D during the AM peak hour and at LOS 

E  during  the  PM peak hour.  The intersection of Royal Palm Boulevard at Rock Island Road currently 

operates at LOS E during the AM and PM peak hours.  At the intersection of Royal Palm Boulevard and 

Sunflower Drive, traffic along the major street (Royal Palm Boulevard) operates at LOS A.  The analysis 

shows that the minor street (Sunflower Drive) currently operates with significant vehicular delays.  Field 

observations regarding side street delays contradict the HCS results.  Field observations, as previously 

described, indicate sufficient gaps are available in the existing traffic stream to allow vehicles to complete 

their desired maneuver with minimal delay.

3.1     Background Growth Analysis 

Traffic  volumes  for  background  traffic  conditions  (future  conditions  without  project  traffic)  were 

determined for the buildout year of the project, anticipated by Year 2016, by applying a growth rate to 

existing volumes.  The growth rate for this study was determined based on a review of fiveyear historical 

traffic volumes obtained for seven (7) count stations near the project site.  The count stations included the 

following: 

 

              Station 867164:  Royal Palm Boulevard east of Riverside Drive 

              Station 867473:  Royal Palm Boulevard west of SR7 

              Station 867475:  Riverside Drive south of Royal Palm Boulevard  

              Station 867476:  Royal Palm Boulevard east of University Drive 

              Station 867593:  Rock Island Road south of Royal Palm Boulevard  

              Station 867662:  Riverside Drive south of Sample Road 

              Station 869166:  Rock Island Road south of Sample Road 

 

The  collected  historical  volume  data  is  included  in  Appendix  C.    Review  of  the  historical  volumes 

indicates a decreasing trend for all roadway segments.  To provide a conservative analysis, a 0.5 percent 

compound growth rate was used for the analysis.

4.4     Intersection Capacity Analysis ­ Total Traffic Conditions 

Intersection  capacity  analysis  was  performed  for  total  traffic  conditions.   Peak  hour  factors  and  truck 

factors were the same as existing conditions.  The intersection capacity analysis worksheets are included 

in Appendix F.  Results of the analysis, summarized in Table 3, indicate that the intersection of Royal 

Palm Boulevard and Riverside Drive will continue to operate at LOS D during the AM peak hour and at 

LOS E during the PM peak hour, with minimal vehicular delay increases of 1.7 seconds per vehicle and 

1.1 seconds per vehicle, respectively.  The intersection of Royal Palm Boulevard at Rock Island Road is 

expected to continue to operate at LOS E during the AM and PM peak hours, with minimal vehicular 

delay increases of 3.7 seconds per vehicle and 4.8 seconds per vehicle, respectively.   

 

At the intersection of Royal Palm Boulevard and Sunflower Drive, traffic along Royal Palm Boulevard 

will operate at LOS A.  Similar to the analysis of existing conditions, significant delays for the minor street 

(Sunflower Drive/Driveway) are projected by the HCS software.  However, sufficient gaps are expected to 

continue  to  be  available  along  Royal  Palm  Boulevard  to  allow  northbound  vehicles  to  complete  their 

desired maneuver with minimal delay.  The southbound volume at Sunflower Drive is projected to be 

significantly  less  (approximately  64  percent  less)  as  compared  to  the  existing  northbound  volume.  

Therefore, it is anticipated that project traffic will exit the site with minimal delay at the project buildout. 

 

4.5     Significance Analysis 

Royal Palm Boulevard is a fourlane, divided roadway that abuts the project site to the south.  Based on 

the Broward County Roadway Capacity and Level of Service Analysis tables, the 2011 Generalized Peak 

Hour  TwoWay  Level  of  Service  capacity  for  Royal  Palm  Boulevard  abutting  the  project  site  is  3,382.  

Broward County guidelines indicate a significance threshold for roadways of three (3) percent.   

 

Based  on  the  trips  anticipated  from  the  proposed  site  for  worst  case  traffic  conditions,  which  occurs 

during the PM peak hour, the maximum project volume along Royal Palm Boulevard is anticipated to be 

43 twoway trips.  This equates to a significance level (project trips divided by the roadway capacity) of 

approximately  1.27  percent,  or  43  percent  of  the  significant  threshold.    Therefore,  the  proposed 

development  is  expected  to  have  minimal/insignificant  impact  on  the  surrounding  roadway  network 

based on Broward County guidelines.

                                   5.0     GATE QUEUING ANALYSIS 
 

The proposed development will be accessed through a gatehouse located at the entrance to the property.  

The gatehouse will accommodate two (2) inbound lanes ­ one visitor/guest entrance lane and one resident 

entrance lane.  Analysis was performed for the visitor and resident entrances to ensure adequate queue 

stacking is being provided within the site to avoid queue backup onto Royal Palm Boulevard. 

 

5.1     Trip Generation Analysis ­ Peak Hour of Generator 

Trip  generation  analysis  was  performed,  based  on  peak  hour  for  the  proposed  development,  during 

weekday,  Saturday  and  Sunday  conditions  to  determine  the  highest  trip  generation.    Results  of  the 

analysis, summarized in Table G1 and included in Appendix G, indicate that the highest inbound traffic 

volume  for  the  site  is  anticipated  to  occur  during  the  weekday  PM  peak  hour.    Therefore,  the  gate 

queuing analysis was performed for this peak period.  Excerpts from ITE are attached in Appendix G. 

 

5.2     Gate Handling Capacity 

The technology for resident access (key card/transponders) will provide continuous access.  Visitors will 

use  a  call  box  operation.    The  gate  handling  capacities  for  residents  and  visitors  is  conservatively 

estimated as follows:

 

        Resident PM peak (entering):  80 percent of entering trips = 40 vehicles per hour 

        Gate operation:  30 seconds per interval 

        60 min./hr. x 60 sec./min. = 3,600 sec./hr./1 veh./30 sec. = 120 vehicles/hour per gate 

 

        Visitor PM peak (entering):  20 percent of entering trips = 10 vehicles per hour 

        Call Box operation:  120 seconds per interval 

        60 min./hr. x 60 sec./min. = 3,600 sec./hr./1 veh./120 sec. = 30 vehicles/hour per gate 

 

The  capacity  of  120  vehicles  per  hour  for  residents  and  30  vehicles  per  hour  for  visitors  exceeds  the 

projected peak hour entering volume for each. 




                                                       17  
5.3     Gate Queues 

A queue analysis was performed for the visitor and resident entrance lanes that will serve the proposed 

development.  During the weekday PM peak hour, the proposed development is expected to generate 10 

inbound  and  6  outbound  visitor  trips,  for  a  total  of  16  visitor  trips.   The  proposed  use  is  expected  to 

generate 40 inbound and 22 outbound resident trips, for a total of 62 resident trips.   

 

A  queue  analysis  was  performed  based  on  the  methodology  outlined  in  the  Transportation  and  Land 

Development,  1988,  published  by  ITE.    As  previously  mentioned,  the  average  service  capacity  is 

anticipated to be 30 vehicles per hour per lane for visitors and 120 vehicles per hour per lane for residents. 

 In an effort to ensure that the available storage length for the gate facility is not exceeded, thus creating 

spillback  onto  the  adjacent  roadway,  a  95  percent  confidence  level  was  applied.    Excerpts  from  the 

published data are included in Appendix G. 

 

Results of the visitor and resident queue analyses, summarized in Table G2 and Table G3, respectively, 

included  in  Appendix  G,  indicate  that  a  maximum  queue  of  one  vehicle  is  expected  in  each  lane,  in 

addition  to  the  vehicle  in  each  lane  that  is  being  serviced.   The  visitor  and  resident  lanes  will  provide 

storage for approximately five (5) vehicles and seven (7) vehicles, respectively, before extending to the 

first  potential  vehicular  conflict  along  Royal  Palm  Boulevard.    Therefore,  sufficient  storage  is  being 

provided on the site to avoid queue spillback onto Royal Palm Boulevard.

McMahon has completed a transportation analysis for the redevelopment of a parcel of land located on 

the  north  side  of  Royal  Palm  Boulevard  just  east  of  Riverside  Drive,  in  the  City  of  Margate.   The  site 

currently  includes  the  First  Presbyterian  Church.    The  proposed  development,  with  an  anticipated 

buildout year of 2016, will include 125 residential townhomes. 

 

Based on the analysis, the following can be summarized/concluded: 

         The capacity analysis at the study intersections was performed with no trip credit taken for 

             the  existing  church  on  the  site.   Therefore,  the  site  was  assumed  to  be  vacant  for  analysis 

             purpose to provide a conservative analysis.   

         The intersection of Royal Palm Boulevard and Riverside Drive currently operates at LOS D 

             during the AM peak hour and at LOS E during the PM peak hour.  At the project buildout, the 

             intersection is expected to continue to operate at LOS D during the AM peak hour and LOS E 

             during the PM peak hour, with minimal vehicular delay increases of 1.7 seconds per vehicle 

             and 1.1 seconds per vehicle, respectively.   

         The intersection of Royal Palm Boulevard at Rock Island Road currently operates at LOS E 

             during the AM and PM peak hours.  At the project buildout, the intersection is expected to 

             continue  to  operate  at  LOS  E  during  the  AM  and  PM  peak  hours,  with  minimal  vehicular 

             delay increases of 3.7 seconds per vehicle and 4.8 seconds per vehicle, respectively.   

         At the intersection of Royal Palm Boulevard and Sunflower Drive, traffic along Royal Palm 

             Boulevard  currently  operates  at  LOS  A.   At  the  project  buildout,  Royal  Palm  Boulevard  is 

             anticipated to continue to operate at LOS A, with no vehicular delay during the AM peak hour 

             and with a minimal vehicular delay of 0.2 seconds per vehicle during the PM peak hour. 

         Based on field observations, a typical vehicular queue of two (2) vehicles or less is anticipated 

             for the northbound and southbound travel movements from Sunflower Drive to Royal Palm 

             Boulevard.  Sufficient gaps are expected in the traffic stream along Royal Palm Boulevard to 

             allow vehicles to complete their desired maneuver with minimal delay. 

         Broward County guidelines indicate a significance threshold for roadways of three (3) percent.
