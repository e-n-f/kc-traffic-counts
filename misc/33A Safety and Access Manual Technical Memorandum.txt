BACKGROUND AND PURPOSE

The intersection of County Road 33A (CR 33A) and Election House Road, including the adjacent intersection
of North Columbus Street and Election House Road, in Fairfield County has a history of complaints and
concerns dating back to when CR 33A was U.S. Route 33. In fact, at one time, the Ohio Department of
Transportation had considered closing this access point (Election House Road at CR 33A) entirely or making
it a right-in/right-out only by closing the median in front of the intersection. Based on these historical
concerns, reported crashes, and public comments, the Fairfield County Engineer's office has retained the
services of Burgess & Niple, Inc. to prepare a study of the intersection and its surrounding area.

The purpose of the study is to identify the problem and develop a solution that both corrects any current
deficiencies and safety problems at the intersection, and provides for a long-term solution that accommodates
current and future land use plans in the corridor.

Stakeholder Involvement

Appendix A includes notes and attendee lists from three meetings that were held in conjunction with this
study. These meetings were held to gain input on existing conditions, problems, and concerns; future land
development and growth plans; ideas for improvement alternatives; and input and reaction to alternatives
presented. The discussions at the stakeholder meetings highly influenced the direction and outcome of this
study.

Study Area

The focus of this study is on the intersections of Election House Road with CR 33A and North Columbus
Street. However, in order to properly account for land use impacts and operations at adjacent intersections,
the study area includes a larger corridor of CR 33A from Victor Road to Ety Road. Figure 1 illustrates the
study area.

PROBLEMS, CONCERNS, ISSUES

Crash History

Figure 2 on the next page illustrates the recent crash experience (2005-2007) at the intersection of Election
House Road with CR 33A and North Columbus Street. The crash rate for the intersection of Election House
Road and CR 33A was calculated to be 0.35 crashes per million entering vehicles. This rate is not technically
considered to be a high crash rate when compared statewide and is actually about average for this type of
intersection. However, given the clear crash pattern involving vehicles turning left from Election House Road
onto CR 33A (8 of the 13), there is a certainly an identifiable problem that should be addressed at this
intersection. This pattern will likely become worse as traffic volumes grow due to land development in and
adjacent to the corridor. This crash pattern is likely caused by the combination of the long southbound to
eastbound left turn movement that must traverse two westbound lanes and a wide median, and the large traffic
volume (small number of gaps in traffic) on CR 33A. Also contributing to the crash problem is the sight
distance problems that result when an eastbound CR 33A vehicle is waiting to turn left onto Election House
Road, blocking the view of traffic in adjacent eastbound through lanes to vehicles turning left from Election
House Road.

Observed Capacity Deficiency

Stakeholders have indicated that there are significant backups for southbound Ety Road traffic at CR 33A.
The performed capacity analysis does not indicate this result. However, if the north leg of the intersection is
significantly over capacity, then this analysis result is logical since the traffic counts obtained by the County
would only include those cars that actually made it through the intersection, not the actual demand, thus
skewing the results of the analysis.

Spacing between CR 33A and North Columbus Street

The spacing between North Columbus Street and CR 33A is very problematic. North Columbus Street backs
up at times because it is blocked by vehicles turning from Election House Road to CR 33A. Stakeholders also
indicated that they observe many "near misses" due to the complicated operations that result from the close
spacing. The spacing is drastically deficient when compared to the recommended spacing for intersections.
According to the ODOT State Highway Access Management Manual (2001), the recommended spacing of
between two intersections on a roadway like Election House Road (assuming access category IV), is to be an
absolute minimum of one-quarter mile and is preferred to be one-half mile (assuming urban conditions).

Other Issues/Concerns

Other pertinent issues and concerns raised by stakeholders include:

·   EMS officials indicated that access across CR 33A from Election House Road to Elm Street (Colonial
    Estates) is critical, and that several runs per day are made to that neighborhood.

·   Turning onto CR 33A from Ask Powersports is very difficult because of a very busy CR 33A. Capacity
    analysis of that driveway entrance was not part of this study.

·   It was noted by several stakeholders that eastbound CR 33A vehicles turn left at Election House Road and
    cut through on North Columbus Street to avoid waiting at the signal Ety Road intersection to turn left.

INITIAL LONG-TERM ALTERNATIVE CONCEPTS

Based on stakeholder feedback, existing deficiencies, anticipated land use, and projected traffic volumes,
three initial long-term improvement concepts were developed to attempt to provide a viable transportation
network in the study area. The purpose of these concepts is to eliminate existing deficiencies and illustrate
important interconnections between properties. Detailed engineering traffic analyses were not conducted on
these concepts initially. Rather, schematics of these concepts were first presented to the stakeholder group for
review and comment to identify any "fatal flaws" that would render a concept unacceptable. Acceptable
concepts were then forwarded in the process and analyzed in more detail. The concepts are as shown in
Figures 7-9on the following pages along with advantages and disadvantages of each.

It should be noted that North Columbus Street is shown as closed/abandoned east of Election House Road
since its spacing with CR 33A is a major deficiency and is expected to become an even bigger problem as
development occurs and traffic grows in the corridor. It should also be noted that it is assumed that capacity
improvements will be made in the future by the City of Lancaster on Ety Road between CR 33A and North
Columbus Street to correct the capacity deficiency there. This study does not specifically address solutions
for that section of roadway.

Based on the "fatal flaw" analysis, only Concept 1 was carried forward from this step in the study. Concept 2
was eliminated because there was a general consensus among the stakeholders that eliminating left turn access
for the entire corridor would be detrimental to the economic vitality of the area. Additionally, Concept 2,
with only two intersections, Victor Road and Ety Road, providing access to CR 33A for future development,
will almost certainly fail from a traffic operations standpoint even if improvements are made to them (this is
verified in later in the process). Concept 3 was dropped because the two intersections (CR 33A and North
Columbus Street), in such close proximity, were not expected to work operationally.

Additional Analysis of Concept 1

2030 traffic estimates were prepared for Concept 1 and modeled in Synchro software. The results were that
this concept would be well over its capacity and would fail by 2030. The analysis assumed that significant
improvements would be made at each intersection, but no reasonable intersection configurations could be
identified that would handle the projected traffic load. The conclusion was that based on the projected
development in the study area, additional access to CR 33A will be required to "spread-out" the traffic to
minimize traffic volumes at each intersection.

RECOMMENDED LONG-TERM SOLUTION

Figure 10 on the following page illustrates the recommended long-term solution. This long-term solution has
the following characteristics:

    ·   Four, approximately evenly spaced, signalized access points to CR 33A to accommodate future
        development. These four signalized access points includes a new access located between North
        Columbus Street and Ety Road. While the signal spacing is not preferred (1/2 mile is preferred), the
        resultant spacing is approximately ¼ mile and meets the minimum recommended spacing for an
        urban roadway (Reference: ODOT State Highway Access Management Manual). It is recognized
        that when the proposed development occurs, the nature of this corridor will be substantially changed
        into an urban commercial corridor, and the proposed intersection spacing is consistent with that type
        of environment.

    ·   A key to the solution is that as properties develop in the corridor, a network of roadway
        interconnections that, in concept, match the connections shown in the figure (in red) be made to
        distribute the traffic growth across the four signalized access points at CR 33A.

    ·   An important assumption is that the City of Lancaster will upgrade Ety Road between CR 33A and
        North Columbus Street (shown in yellow in the figure).

    ·   Realignment of the existing Elm Street to align with Election House Road at an intersection with CR
        33A to create a safe, organized, signalized intersection serving both crossroads.

    ·   Closure/abandonment of the section of North Columbus Street between Election House Road and the
        Bob Boyd east entrance to eliminate the problem of the closely spaced intersections along Election
        House Road.

    ·   A raised median along CR 33A between signalized intersections (shown in black on figure) to
        eliminate any left turn movements to and from CR 33A except for the four signal controlled
        intersections to provide good access management which will result in a safer and more efficient
        roadway.

    ·   Consolidation of driveways on Election House Road just north of CR 33A to improve traffic flow and
        safety.

It should be noted that since this is old U.S. 33, ODOT likely has limited access right-of-way to this section of
roadway. Any new or relocated access points, as proposed in this long-term plan, will likely require approval
from ODOT and the Federal Highway Administration.

MEDIUM-TERM SOLUTION

Two medium-term concepts were evaluated. The purpose of evaluating these concepts is to attempt to
identify a project at the CR 33A/Election House/North Columbus Street area that could be completed within
the next five to ten years that would improve safety and eliminate deficiencies in the corridor. Another
important criterion is that the medium-term solution must be consistent with, and lead logically into, the long-
term recommendations.

Medium-term Concept 1

Figure 12 illustrates Medium-term Concept 1, which is intended to maintain North Columbus Street's
connection to Election House Road by signalizing the intersection including the integration of North
Columbus Street. This concept also eliminates the "offset" condition of Elm Street and Election House road
and would provide an excellent EMS route to serve Colonial Estates. Traffic signal operation was show to
the stakeholder group at the third meeting by use of the software SimTraffic. While this option would likely
have the capacity to accommodate today's traffic along with a minimal amount of growth, it will eventually
fail due to the inefficiency of serving North Columbus Street in the signal cycle. Eventually, to accommodate
future growth, the North Columbus Street connection will have to be severed from Election House Road.
Additionally, there are significant concerns with this concept about driver error and confusion resulting in
traffic backups into CR 33A, which could result in a serious crash. Because of these deficiencies and
concerns, Medium-term Concept 1 is not recommended for implementation.

If significant development and more substantial intersection improvements are not expected to occur within
the next 3-5 years then it appears this improvement could provide a significant safety benefit and should be
implemented.
