                                                                                                      TRAFFIC EVALUATION
             Vanasse Hangen Brustlin, Inc. (VHB) has prepared this traffic evaluation in support of next phase of the
             previously approved expansion (the Project) of the Lahey Hospital & Medical Center (Lahey) located at 41
             Burlington Mall Road in Burlington, Massachusetts. Specifically, the evaluation focuses on the traffic
             operations associated with the construction of a new median break on Burlington Mall Road at the westerly
             Lahey driveway and installation of a new traffic signal at the intersection.
             The memorandum includes an evaluation of the existing traffic conditions; future conditions without the
             Project; an estimate of future traffic volumes at Lahey's driveways on Burlington Mall Road without and with
             the signalized median break; and related traffic capacity analyses.
             Overall, the evaluation indicates that creating a signalized median break at the westerly driveway provides
             improved access to the relocated Emergency Department (ED) with minimal impact to the traffic operations on
             Burlington Mall Road. In fact, providing signalized left turn access for Lahey traffic at a second driveway on
             Burlington Mall Road will help reduce capacity constraints at Lahey's main driveway and improve traffic
             circulation within the Lahey campus.

             BACKGROUND AND CURRENT DEVELOPMENT PHASE
             The October 7, 2004 Planning Board Special Permit granted Lahey the permission to construct a phased
             expansion of the campus. The Project also underwent MEPA and MassDOT review in 2004/2005, resulting in
             a MEPA Certificate dated January 14, 2005 and a Massachusetts Department of Transportation (MassDOT)
             Access Permit dated February 7, 2006. Local and state submittals leading up to the above stated approvals
             included detailed traffic analyses of the full build-out condition, which included approximately 331,000 square
             feet (sf) of new hospital space on the campus. Since the issuance of the prior approvals, Lahey has constructed
             approximately 168,000 sf of new medical space.
             The current phase of the campus expansion project includes the construction of an approximately 49,000 sf
             ED, approximately 61,000 sf future oncology department and approximately 53,000 sf of shell space, which
             makes up the remaining approximately 163,000 sf of new medical space that has been approved but not yet
             constructed.
             Access to the campus will continue to be provided via the existing driveways along Burlington Mall Road and
             Kimball Avenue. Since the focus of this evaluation is to analyze and document the effect of a new signalized
             median break that was not previously contemplated at the location of the westerly Lahey driveway on
             Burlington Mall Road, the analysis presented in this memorandum includes the two Lahey driveways that are
             affected by the change; i.e., the west driveway and the main driveway on Burlington Mall Road.

    EXISTING CONDITIONS
    The following section provides a brief description of the roadway and intersection characteristics considered in
    the traffic analysis.

    Study Area Roadway
    Burlington Mall Road is a town-owned, four-lane, median-divided roadway extending from its intersection
    with Middlesex Turnpike in the west to Cambridge Street in the east, a length of approximately one mile. It
    provides access to several office developments and retail/commercial establishments, including Burlington
    Mall, as well as to the Lahey campus. Separate turning lanes and traffic signals are provided along Burlington
    Mall Road at all major intersections including at South Bedford Street and at the Lahey main entrance. The
    posted speed limit along this roadway near the site is 35 miles per hour (mph).

    Study Area Intersections
    Burlington Mall Road at the Lahey Westerly Driveway
    The Lahey westerly driveway intersects Burlington Mall Road from the south to form a T-type unsignalized
    intersection. The Burlington Mall Road approach is the two eastbound lanes, separated from the two
    westbound lanes by a wide median. The wide driveway is limited to a right-in/right-out operation only and is
    under stop-sign control. Sidewalks exist along Burlington Mall Road. Land uses near the intersection consist
    mainly of the Lahey campus on the south side and office development on the north side of the intersection.
    Burlington Mall Road at Lahey Main Driveway/ Executive Place Driveway
    The Lahey main driveway and Executive Place driveway intersect Burlington Mall Road from the south and
    north, respectively, to form a four-way signalized intersection. The Burlington Mall Road eastbound approach
    is comprised of a left-turn lane, two through lanes, and a right-turn lane; the westbound approach is comprised
    of a left-turn lane, a through lane, and a shared through-right lane. The Lahey Clinic driveway is four-lane,
    median divided with two exit lanes. The Executive Place driveway is median divided at the intersection and
    provides a wide, unmarked exit that can accommodate two lanes, and a wide entering lane. Sidewalks exist
    along Burlington Mall Road; a crosswalk, operating on an exclusive pedestrian traffic signal phase, is located
    across Burlington Mall Road on the west side of the intersection. Land uses near the intersection consist
    mainly of the Lahey campus on the south side and mixed-office development on the north side of the
    intersection.

ACCESS IMPROVEMENTS ­ LAHEY WESTERLY DRIVEWAY
As described earlier, the Lahey westerly driveway currently operates under stop sign control, with a right-in,
right-out configuration, on a median-divided section of Burlington Mall Road. It essentially serves as a
secondary driveway for service and delivery vehicles and some employees. A majority of vehicles currently
access Lahey via the main signalized driveway on Burlington Mall Road. The main driveway also currently
provides direct access to the Emergency Department.

A review of the Site Plans indicates that most of the future development is planned in the southwesterly
quadrant of the campus. Among other components, the plan calls for substantially expanding and relocating
the Emergency Department to the southwesterly quadrant. After a careful review of vehicular access needs,
future parking locations and emergency vehicle access, it was determined that the Lahey westerly driveway
will need to be enhanced and converted to a full access driveway by creating a signalized median break on
Burlington Mall Road to better serve the future Lahey campus and to help distribute traffic loading between
multiple driveways, rather than a single signalized driveway. On-campus circulation of emergency, patient,
visitor and employee vehicles will also be improved by providing two full access curb-cuts on Burlington Mall
Road.

Figure 7 shows the proposed configuration of the signalized median break contemplated for the westerly
driveway intersection. The plan includes the following elements:

           A break in the center median on Burlington Mall Road directly across from the westerly driveway;
           Addition of a westbound left-turn lane on Burlington Mall Road to provide a safe storage area left
           turning vehicles, outside of the through vehicular flow;
           Installation of a fully actuated traffic signal at the intersection that will be coordinated with the system
           that will be implemented by the NEEP project;
           Construction of a signalized pedestrian crosswalk across Burlington Mall Road;
           Implementation of preemption capability at the intersection to facilitate safe and efficient emergency
           vehicle travel through the intersection; and,
           Installation of signage improvements to reinforce prohibited movements at the intersection.

In addition to attracting a majority of the new development traffic, the signalized median break is expected to
draw some of the existing traffic that is currently uses the Lahey main driveway. Build traffic volume networks
showing the redistribution of peak hour traffic volumes between the two driveways are shown in Figures 8 and
9. Capacity analysis results for the study locations with the new traffic signal are summarized in Tables 6 and
7.

Signal Warrant Analysis
Traffic signal Warrant analyses were prepared to determine if the projected traffic volumes at the intersection
exceed the thresholds for the installation of a traffic signal. The Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices2
(MUTCD) is the established standard for Warrant analyses. The Warrants consider the roadway geometry,
traffic volume entering the intersection, and speeds. Specifically, the traffic projections were evaluated for
following three volume-based Warrants:

           Warrant 1 (Eight Hour Vehicular Volume) ­ Warrant 1 is based on any eight hours of a day where
           the traffic entering the intersection reaches a threshold that warrants considering signal control.
           Warrant 2 (Four Hour Vehicular Volume) ­ Warrant 2 is for any four hours of a day.
           Warrant 3 (Peak Hour) ­ Warrant 3 is for the peak hour of any given day.
The traffic signal Warrant analysis indicates that the estimated traffic volumes at intersection would satisfy all
three traffic volume-based Warrants in the future for the installation of a traffic signal.
