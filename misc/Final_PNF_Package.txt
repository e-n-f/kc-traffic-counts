                        Additional Public Input Summary

Background

As part of the Town's ongoing efforts to improve the conditions along Main Street in West Chatham, a
Public Informational Meeting was held on 10/20/08 to "kickoff" the design process for potential
improvements to the Barn Hill Road / Main Street and George Ryder Road / Main Street intersections and
Main Street in the West Chatham corridor. Building off the analysis conducted and concepts generated as
part of the West Chatham Grant study process, the Town sought additional public input prior to
submitting a Project Need Form (PNF) to the Massachusetts Highway Department (MHD). A copy of
the presentation from that meeting and a disc containing the West Chatham Grant study are included.

The West Chatham Grant study made the following specific design recommendations:


Route 28 / George Ryder Road Intersection

        ·   Realign George Ryder Rd.                 ·     Reduce Slope George Ryder Road South
        ·   Reduce Curb Radii                        ·     Planting Center Median
        ·   Crosswalks / Handicap Ramps              ·     Bike Trail Signage Access
        ·   Relocate Curb Cuts                       ·     Sightline Improvements


Route 28 Corridor

       ·    Reduce Lane Width                        ·     Speed Limit Reduced
       ·    Create Center Lane Medians               ·     Bike Lane/Signage
       ·    Pedestrian Crossings                     ·     Curbing/Driveway Aprons
       ·    Roadway Drainage                         ·     Sidewalks
       ·    Plantings                                ·     Street Furniture & Amenities


Route 28 / Barn Hill Road Intersection

       ·    Relocate throat of Barn Hill             ·     Planted Center Median
       ·    Crosswalks / Handicap Ramps              ·     Relocate Curb Cuts




                                             Page 1 of 2
Additional Input from 10/20/08 Meeting


Short Term Actions

Approximately sixty participants at this meeting concurred with the need to make improvements in the
project area and supported the above recommendations. Additionally, a desire to implement interim
measures including: installing seasonal traffic lights, improving signage, improving pavement markings
and a reduced speed zone were strongly expressed to address safety and traffic issues.


Additional Design Considerations

In addition to the above recommendations and the West Chatham Grant study design recommendations
the group also expressed additional emphasis on the following:

   ·   Design respective of neighborhood and historical character

   ·   Attractive and functional streetscape

   ·   Accommodation of bicycle travel / connection with Bike Trail

   ·   Improved public amenities and conditions for pedestrians

   ·   Exploration of roundabouts as an alternative to traffic signals

   ·   Consideration of the impact of proposed PD Annex project on George Ryder Road intersection

   ·   Reduction of traffic accidents / Improved vehicular safety

   ·   Improved operation for residents in affected neighborhoods

   ·   Accelerated design and approval process
