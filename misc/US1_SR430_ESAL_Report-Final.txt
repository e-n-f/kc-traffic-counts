INTRODUCTION
Ghyabi & Associates, Inc. (G&A) has been retained by the Florida Department of Transportation,
District Five to provide transportation engineering services in association with the Project Traffic for
PD&E and Design contract. This Technical Memorandum for 18 Kip Equivalent Single Axle Load
(ESAL) forecasts was prepared as part of the services covered under this contract pursuant to the letter
of Authorization for Work Order Number 182 dated November, 2004.

STUDY OBJECTIVE
The objective of this project is to develop 18 Kip ESAL forecasts for use by the Florida Department of
Transportation District Five for the intersection evaluation at US1 @ SR 430 (Mason Avenue), Project
ID Number 240992-1, located in Volusia County, Florida. The scope of this Technical Memorandum
entails the development of future traffic forecasts and evaluation of the characteristics and basic
operational conditions of the corridor during the design life of the project.

METHODOLOGY
The methodology prepared for the development of this Technical Memorandum is consistent with the
Project Traffic Forecasting Procedure and Handbook (Topic No. 525-030-120-g) published by the
Florida Department of Transportation. The methodology covers the following topics:

    ·   Collect available traffic count information from the Department's historical traffic count records
        and from actual field count data, review previous studies, traffic characteristics, and other
        relevant data.

    ·   Based on the data collection process, estimate future travel characteristics of the corridor. These
        characteristics include Design Hour Volume factor (K), Directional Design Hour Volume factor
        (D), and Design Truck factor (T).

    ·   Develop future year traffic volume forecasts for the corridor based on trend analysis of historical
        traffic counts and/or officially adopted travel demand models (FSUTMS).

    ·   Evaluate the future year traffic volume forecasts based on capacity to determine whether the
        corridor will operate under constrained or unconstrained capacity conditions.

    ·   Modify the travel characteristics (K-demand) based on constrained or unconstrained operating
        conditions.

    ·   In addition to design year traffic conditions, develop opening year and mid-year traffic volume
        forecasts.

    ·   Provide a Generalized Link Level of Service (LOS) analysis for the corridor.

    ·   Establish a lane equivalency factor (damage factor), lane factor (percentage of trucks in design
        lane) and daily directional split for ESAL forecasting utilizing standard lane factors.

PROJECT LOCATION
The Intersection Evaluation at US1 and SR 430 (Mason Avenue) Project is located in Volusia County,
Florida. While US1 serves as a North/South roadway connector, SR 430 runs East/West. This project is
designated State Project ID Number 240992-1 in the Department's Five Year Work Program. The
project consists of the intersection of US1 and SR 430 (M.P. 1.192). Figure 1 illustrates the project
location.

US1 and SR 430 are both urban 4-lane divided arterials within the project limits. The Intersection
Evaluation Project is scheduled for the year 2010 (opening year). Therefore, the design year is 2030
and the mid-design year is 2020.

DATA COLLECTION
As directed by the Department, Ghyabi & Associates, Inc. conducted two 72-hour vehicle classification
counts, one on the north approach and the other on the east approach of the study intersection. The 72-
hour classification counts were conducted from December 7, 2004 through December 9, 2004. The
vehicle classification count is grouped into three primary vehicle types:
   · Passenger Vehicles ­ Motorcycles, Cars, Vans, and Pick-ups (Class 1 through 3)
   · Buses and Medium Trucks (single unit trucks) ­ Class 4 and 5
   · Heavy and Large Trucks ­ Trucks with one or more trailers (Class 6 through 13)

DESIGN CHARACTERISTICS

The design traffic characteristics for the project shown in Table 1 were developed from the traffic count
data collected in the field and from the FDOT Traffic Information 2003. Measured K, D and T factors
were determined from the vehicle classification counts. These factors were compared with the
respective factors reported in the Florida Department of Transportation database. The recommended
traffic design characteristic values shown in Table 1 were developed based on the comparison of
measured and published K, D and T factors. Based on projected congestion, the lower values
corresponding to the traffic count data collected in the field were selected for K30 and D30.

Using the classification count, an estimated K301 factor was calculated based on the ratio of the median
seasonal factor for the highest 13 weeks (peak season) and the median seasonal factor for the lowest 13
weeks (non-peak season), which can be found in Appendix B. Based on these calculations, a ratio of
1.08 was applied to the measured "K" to develop the estimated K30. The measured K values from the
classification counts are 7.6% and 7.8% for locations 1 and 2, respectively. Thus, the estimated K30
values are 8.21% along US1 and 8.42% along SR 430. The K30 value of 10.19% was obtained from the
RCI database for both US1 and SR 430.

The measured "D" based on the classification counts are 50.7% along US1 and 52.4% along SR 430.
The "D" obtained from the RCI database is 55.15% for both roadways.

The measured "Tdaily" based on the classification count is 3.28% along US1 and 2.56% along SR 430.
The "Tdaily" obtained from the RCI database is 7.53% along US1 and 4.15% along SR 430.


The recommended K30 values for the project traffic are 8.21% along US1 and 8.42% along SR 430and
the recommended D30 values are 50.7% along US1 and 52.4% along SR 430, as obtained based on the
classification counts. The values for K30 and D30 are not within the recommended limits shown in the
FDOT Project Traffic Forecasting Handbook for unconstrained sites. Also, in order to be conservative,
the higher value of 7.53% along US 1 and 4.15% along SR 430 from the FDOT database has been
recommended for Tdaily.

LEVEL OF SERVICE ANALYSIS

For the purpose of projecting the future volume, the counts performed by G&A were selected.
Operating conditions, expressed as levels of service (LOS), were determined based on the comparison
between traffic volumes and roadway capacity.

Roadway capacity was established from the Generalized Level of Service Volume Tables provided in
the 2002 FDOT Quality/Level of Service Handbook. Tables 4-1 and 4-7 were utilized. For the purpose
of the LOS analysis US1 and SR 430 was defined as State Two-Way Arterials, Class 1, 4-lane divided.
For ease of reference, copies of all relevant FDOT tables used in this study are located in Appendix E.
The counts taken for this study were used as the basis for future projections to provide an estimate of
travel demand. LOS was estimated for existing conditions, opening, mid-design and design years. As
shown in Figure 2, US1 currently operates at a LOS C and will continue to do so till the opening year,
but will operate at a LOS F from the mid-design and the design year. On the other hand, SR 430
currently operates at a LOS B and will continue to do so through the design year.

Turning movement counts were performed on the intersections of US 1 at SR 430 during the AM peak
and PM peak periods. The analysis was performed to determine the lane configuration and storage
length for the turn lanes required at this intersection to operate at a level of service D or better during
Design Year (2030). The intersection was analyzed by applying the growth rate calculated from the
Traffic Projections section of this report (1%). The volumes were grown to the opening (2010), mid-
design (2020), and design (2030) years. The Existing and Projected Traffic Volumes are shown on
figure 3.

configuration at the intersection of US 1 at SR 430 is "D" for the opening (2010), mid-design (2020)
year and for the design (2030) year also. The existing and proposed lane configurations are shown on
Figure 4. HCS analysis was also conducted for an additional through lane only (i.e., three through lanes)
along US 1 for the design (2030) year. However, the intersection operates at lower LOS "E" under this
configuration. The HCS output can be found in Appendix F, we were also able to determine storage
lengths for the turn lanes. The minimum required storage lengths can be found in Table 8. It should be
