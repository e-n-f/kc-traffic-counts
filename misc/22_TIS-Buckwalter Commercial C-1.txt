INTRODUCTION
Parcel C-1 of the Buckwalter Commercial site is being planned for development with
approximately 8,740 square feet of commercial space. For the initial development, access is
planned as a right in right, out driveway on US 278. Interconnectivity is envisioned as the
surrounding parcels develop.

The site is anticipated to generate over 50 peak hour trips; this impact study is completed in
accordance with the Town of Bluffton traffic impact study guidelines. The limits of the study
will include the driveway access and the Buckwalter Parkway / US 278 intersection. A site
location map and conceptual plan are attached.


EXISTING CONDITIONS
US 278 and Buckwalter Pkwy is a 4-leg signalized intersection. US 278 is a 4-lane median
divided arterial that makes up the eastbound and westbound approaches. Buckwalter Pkwy is the
northbound approach, Berkeley Hall the southbound. Dual left turn lanes are provided for the
westbound (US 278) and northbound (Buckwalter) approaches. Each approach provides a
channelized right turn lane. The side street approaches to US 278 operate under split phasing.

Current peak hour turning movement counts at the Buckwalter Pkwy / US 278 intersection were
provided by the Beaufort County Engineering Department. Several days of data were provided;
the counts used in this study are the most recent and represent the highest intersection volume
(PM peak). Capacity analyses are completed based on the existing lane geometry to determine the
current Level of Service. The existing Levels of Service are shown in Table 1.

       Trips generated by the proposed development are estimated using the standard methodology and
       equations in the Institute of Transportation Engineers (ITE), Trip Generation, 8th Edition, 2008.

       use category is applied.

       establishments that is planned, developed, owned, and managed as a unit. A shopp ing center's
       composition is related to its market area in terms of size, location and type of store.... Some of
       these centers contained non-merchandising facilities, such as office buildings, movie theatres,

       DISTRIBUTION AND ASSIGNMENT
       The site-generated trips are assigned to the roadway network to determine the future traffic
       impacts. Initially, the only access will be via a right in, right out driveway. All of the trips will
       be assigned to the one access. Pass by trips will be subtracted from the through movement at the
       C-1 entrance; only the "new" trips will be assigned to the Buckwalter Parkway intersection.


       ACCESS SPACING/ DESIGN
       The proposed driveway is approximately +-1,500 feet east of the Buckwalter Parkway
       intersection on US 278 (location map attached), and thus could be installed in accordance with
       the US 278 access management plan. It is understood that this driveway will remain as a right-in,
       right-out access; no median break on US 278 will be installed in the future.

       Since more than 40 peak hour right turns are anticipated, a right turn lane is warranted based on
       SCDOT criteria (Roadway Design Manual, Figure 15.5B). Since the right turn volume is
       anticipated to be less than 150 peak hour vehicles, a 100' storage length (with appropriate entry
       radius and channelization) should be sufficient (Roadway Design Manual, Figure 15.5I). Based
       on the speed limit on US 278, the taper length should be 200 feet (Roadway Design Manual,
       Figure 15.5H).


       INTERCONNECTIVITY
       The site plan includes a potential future connection to the upland areas immediately to the west.
       This connection could eventually provide cross access to Buckwalter Parkway.

CONCLUSIONS/ RECOMMENDATIONS
This traffic study analyzes the potential traffic impacts associated with the development of Parcel
C-1 of the Buckwalter Commercial development.

Development of Parcel C-1 should not significantly affect traffic operations at the Buckwalter
Parkway / US 278 intersection, or on US 278 in general. The proposed access can be installed, as
a right-in, right-out driveway, in accordance with US 278 access management standards.

To accommodate the proposed development, a right turn lane on US 278 should be installed.
Based on the anticipated traffic volume and speed limit, the turn lane should include 100 feet of
storage and a 200 foot taper.
